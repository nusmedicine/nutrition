# Guideline register

> Web-grounded by the research pass (2026-06-30). Singapore-first, then international.
> ⚠ **Currency notes** (from verification): My Healthy Plate dates to **2014** and the HPB-MOH
> Obesity CPG to **2016** (aging anchors — date them honestly in teaching); the Nutri-Grade
> extension to other categories (~2027) is **provisional**; check the **ADA Standards of Care
> 2025/2026** edition before citing for a 2026 course.

## Singapore (HPB / MOH / ACE)

### My Healthy Plate (Quarter-Quarter-Half) — HPB, 2014 (page refreshed 2024)
Patient-facing visual food guide. <https://www.healthhub.sg/live-healthy/balanced-diet-qqh-concept>
- Each meal: ¼ wholegrains/wholemeal staples, ¼ protein, ½ fruit & vegetables.
- Choose water over sugary drinks; use healthier oils; stay active.
- Proportional/motivational (no fixed calorie counts); adaptable to local cuisines.

### Nutri-Grade Beverage Labelling — HPB/MOH, 2022/2023
Mandatory front-of-pack A–D grading by sugar and saturated fat. <https://www.healthhub.sg/programmes/nutrition-hub/nutri-grade-mark>
- Graded by the **worse** of sugar or saturated fat per 100 ml. Sugar: A ≤1 g, B >1–5 g, C >5–10 g, D >10 g. Saturated fat: A ≤0.7 g, B >0.7–1.2 g, C >1.2–2.8 g, D >2.8 g.
- Grade C and D must display the mark; Grade D faces an advertising prohibition.
- Rollout: prepacked/dispensers Dec 2022; freshly prepared (bubble tea, kopi/teh) Dec 2023; extension to other categories ~2027 *(provisional)*.

### Singapore Dietary Guidelines & National Nutrition Survey 2022 — HPB/MOH, 2022/2023
National dietary guidelines (adults & seniors) + population monitoring. <https://www.moh.gov.sg/newsroom/national-health-surveys-highlight-need-to-focus-on-healthy-diets-and-lifestyles/>
- NNS 2022: only **~4%** of staples consumed were wholegrains (vs ~30% recommended).
- **~9 in 10** residents exceeded the daily sodium recommendation.
- Protein intake largely adequate (>3 in 4 met recommendations); separate seniors' guidelines exist.

### Healthier Dining Programme / Lower-Calorie Hawker Food — HPB, 2022/2023
Nutrition guidelines & healthier-choice ID for food service / hawker settings. <https://www.hpb.gov.sg/docs/default-source/default-document-library/HDP-Nutrition-Guidelines-Updated-August-2022.pdf>
- Promote lower-calorie hawker options (e.g. thunder-tea rice/lei cha, sliced-fish soup, clear-soup yong tau foo).
- Practical swaps: less rice, less gravy, skin removed, water/lower-sugar drinks.
- Operationalises dietary guidelines in a hawker-dominated eating environment.

### ACE Clinical Guidance: Type 2 Diabetes (non-insulin medications) — ACE-HTA/MOH, 2023
Locally contextualised T2DM guidance *(signposted at first-year level)*. <https://www.ace-hta.gov.sg/docs/default-source/acgs/acg-t2dm-personalising-medications.pdf>
- Lifestyle measures first; metformin first-line pharmacotherapy when targets unmet.
- SGLT-2 inhibitors or GLP-1 RAs when cardiorenal risk reduction needed.
- ≥ annual review of HbA1c, BP, weight/BMI, lipids, renal & complication screening.

### ACE Clinical Guidance: Lipid Management / Cardiovascular Risk — ACE-HTA/MOH, 2023
Risk-stratified lipid/CV-risk guidance (Singapore-modified Framingham). <https://www.ace-hta.gov.sg/healthcare-professionals/ace-repository-for-clinical-guidelines/lipid-management-focus-on-cardiovascular-risk/>
- Risk-stratify with the SG-modified Framingham Risk Score; <10% risk emphasises lifestyle & education.
- Aim for >50% LDL-C reduction on maximally tolerated statins for most patients.
- LDL-C targets stratified by CV-risk category; add ezetimibe when targets unmet / statin not tolerated.

### Asian-specific BMI cut-offs & HPB-MOH Obesity CPG — HPB/MOH, 2016 (cut-offs)/2023
Lower (Asian) BMI action points + obesity guidance. <https://www.primarycarepages.sg/healthier-sg/care-protocols/preventive-health-care-protocols/body-mass-index-control>
- Asian cut-offs: low risk 18.5–22.9, moderate/overweight 23.0–27.4, high risk/obese **≥27.5** (two units stricter than WHO 25/30).
- Lifestyle/behavioural modification first-line; pharmacotherapy adjunct at higher BMI when lifestyle fails.
- BMI control embedded in Healthier SG preventive care.

### War on Diabetes & Diabetes Screening Pathways — MOH, 2016/2019/2022
National diabetes policy umbrella + primary-care screening. <https://www.moh.gov.sg/newsroom/diabetes-the-war-continues/>
- Umbrella for Nutri-Grade, Healthier Dining/Choice, ACE guidances, Healthier SG.
- Screening: FPG first-line; OGTT if FPG ~6.1–6.9 mmol/L; HbA1c endorsed as an alternative first step since Mar 2019.
- Ethnic disparities: Indian & Malay residents have higher prevalence; Asians develop diabetes at younger ages and lower BMIs.

## International

### WHO Healthy Diet — WHO, 2020
Normative population guidance synthesising systematic reviews. <https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/healthy-diet>
- Free sugars <10% of energy (ideally <5%); saturated fat <10%; trans fat <1%; total fat <30%.
- Salt <5 g/day (sodium <2 g/day).
- Emphasise fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts, whole grains — thresholds that anchor most national guidelines.

### ADA Standards of Care — Medical Nutrition Therapy — ADA, 2024 *(check 2025/2026)*
Annually updated, evidence-graded diabetes nutrition guideline. <https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article-pdf/47/Supplement_1/S77/746953/dc24s005.pdf>
- No single ideal macronutrient distribution; several patterns (Mediterranean, DASH, low-fat, carb-restricted, vegetarian/vegan) acceptable and individualised.
- Refer to individualised MNT delivered by a registered dietitian at diagnosis and across the lifespan.
- Emphasise non-starchy vegetables, minimised added sugar & refined grains, whole foods.

### AHA 2021 Dietary Guidance to Improve Cardiovascular Health — AHA, 2021
Scientific statement defining a heart-healthy pattern. <https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000001031>
- Ten features: balance energy; plenty/variety of fruit & vegetables; whole grains; favour plant protein, fish, low-fat dairy; liquid non-tropical plant oils; minimally-processed over ultra-processed; minimise added sugars & salt; limit/avoid alcohol; apply regardless of where food is prepared; overall patterns over single nutrients.

### Dietary Guidelines for Americans 2020–2025 — USDA/HHS, 2020
National federal dietary policy by life stage. <https://snaped.fns.usda.gov/library/materials/dietary-guidelines-americans-2020-2025>
- Healthy pattern across every life stage; customise nutrient-dense choices to culture & budget.
- Limit added sugars <10% calories, saturated fat <10%, sodium <2,300 mg/day, and alcohol.
- Core: vegetables, fruits, whole grains, dairy, varied protein (beans, nuts, seafood), unsaturated oils.

### ACLM Lifestyle Medicine Core Competencies (2022 Update) — ACLM, 2022
10-domain competency framework; Domain 3 = Nutrition Science, Assessment & Prescription. <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9644148/>
- Assess intake/nutrients of deficit-excess; analyse food labels; compare global dietary guidelines.
- Appraise nutrition studies; write evidence-based nutrition prescriptions; describe culinary medicine.
- Identify when to refer to registered dietitians; teach within an integrated six-pillar frame.

### AfN UK Undergraduate Curriculum in Nutrition for Medical Doctors — AfN (with GMC/AoMRC), 2021
National undergraduate medical-nutrition curriculum; eight topic areas A–H. <https://www.associationfornutrition.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/2021-UK-Undergraduate-Curriculum-in-Nutrition-for-Medical-Doctors-FINAL.pdf>
- Topic areas: nutrition in health/disease; screening/assessment; effect of nutrition status on illness; under- & over-nutrition; specific dietary requirements; **hydration**; health promotion.
- Mapped to 13 core competencies and 11 graduation fundamentals.
- Emphasises knowing **when to refer** to a dietitian/registered nutritionist.
