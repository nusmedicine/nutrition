# Evidence grading & tagging convention

We anchor teaching claims to **guidelines first**, then grade the underlying evidence simply and
consistently. Singapore guidance (HPB / MOH) is prioritised, complemented by international.

## Source tier (what *kind* of source)
- **G** — Clinical practice guideline / official dietary guideline (HPB, MOH, WHO, ADA, AHA…)
- **SR** — Systematic review / meta-analysis
- **RCT** — Randomised controlled trial
- **OBS** — Observational (cohort, case-control, cross-sectional)
- **MECH / EXP** — Mechanistic reasoning or expert opinion

## Certainty (how confident — GRADE-style), for synthesised claims
- **High · Moderate · Low · Very low**

## How to tag
In a dossier's evidence table, record: the claim, the source, an evidence level
(e.g. `G (HPB 2023)` or `SR · Moderate`), and the BibTeX key. Prefer the highest-quality source
available; flag when guidance is extrapolated, contested, or based on weak evidence.

> Rationale: first-year teaching should reflect **guideline consensus**, but students should also
> see *why* we believe something and *how strong* the evidence is. Keep tags lightweight — this is
> a teaching repository, not a formal systematic review.
