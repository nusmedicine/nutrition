## Dietary patterns & cardiovascular disease

### PREDIMED — Mediterranean diet & CVD prevention `estruch2018predimed`
- **Design:** multicentre RCT (Spain), 3 arms (MedDiet + EVOO, MedDiet + nuts, reduced-fat control); 7,447 high-CV-risk adults; median ~4.8 y.
- **Finding:** the two MedDiet arms reduced the primary composite (MI, stroke, CV death) by ~30% vs control.
- **Evidence level:** landmark large RCT.
- **⚠ Teach with caveat:** the 2013 paper was **retracted in 2018 and re-published with reanalysis** (one site randomised by household/clinic, not individual); conclusions held but it is no longer a clean individually-randomised RCT. The ~30% is a **relative** risk reduction on a **stroke-dominated** composite — not "clean RCT proof" of CVD prevention.
- <https://nutritionsource.hsph.harvard.edu/2018/06/22/predimed-retraction-republication/>

### DASH trial — dietary pattern & blood pressure `appel1997dash`
- **Design:** multicentre randomised controlled **feeding** trial; control vs fruits-&-vegetables vs combination DASH; 8 weeks at constant weight & sodium; 459 adults (SBP <160, DBP 80–95).
- **Finding:** DASH lowered BP ~5.5/3.0 mmHg overall and ~11.4/5.5 mmHg in hypertensives — **without** weight loss or sodium reduction. Diet as non-pharmacologic BP therapy.
- **Evidence level:** landmark RCT (controlled feeding; high internal validity).
- <https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJM199704173361601>

### Mediterranean-diet umbrella review / meta-analyses `medUmbrella`
- **Design:** umbrella review of 18 meta-analyses of RCTs (~197,965 participants) + dose-response cohort meta-analyses.
- **Finding:** higher MedDiet adherence consistently associated with lower CVD mortality, MI and (most strongly) stroke; most robust in secondary prevention and T2DM; greater adherence → less incident T2DM.
- **Evidence level:** highest-tier synthesis (umbrella reviews + meta-analyses).
- <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11795232/>

## Diabetes prevention, management & remission

### Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) `dpp2002`
- **Design:** RCT, 3 arms — intensive lifestyle (≥7% weight loss, ≥150 min/wk activity), metformin, placebo; 3,234 adults with prediabetes; mean 2.8 y.
- **Finding:** intensive lifestyle reduced incident T2DM by **58%**, metformin by **31%**, vs placebo.
- **Evidence level:** landmark large RCT (practice-changing).
- **⚠ Teach with caveat:** DPP lifestyle was an **intensive 16-session programme**, not a brief clinic visit; "lifestyle beats the drug" needs the intensity/feasibility caveat (metformin's effect was attenuated, not absent).
- <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1370926/>

### DiRECT — type 2 diabetes remission `lean2018direct`
- **Design:** open-label cluster-RCT in UK primary care; structured total-diet-replacement (~825–853 kcal/day) then stepped food reintroduction vs usual care; 298 adults, T2DM ≤6 y, BMI 27–45, **not on insulin**.
- **Finding:** at 12 months **46%** achieved remission off all glucose-lowering drugs (vs 4% control); ~86% among those losing ≥15 kg; 36% still in remission at 2 years.
- **Evidence level:** landmark RCT (cluster-randomised; practice-influencing).
- **⚠ Teach with caveat:** remission applies to **early (≤6 y), non-insulin-treated** T2DM, BMI 27–45 — not a universal claim.
- <https://www.thelancet.com/article/S0140-6736(17)33102-1/fulltext>

### Look AHEAD — intensive lifestyle in established T2DM `lookahead2013`
- **Design:** multicentre RCT, intensive lifestyle vs diabetes support/education; 5,145 overweight/obese adults with T2DM; median 9.6 y (stopped for futility).
- **Finding:** **no** reduction in CV events (primary endpoint), but many secondary benefits — better glycaemic/lipid control, less medication, less sleep apnoea & depression, improved mobility & QoL.
- **Evidence level:** landmark large RCT (null primary result — a good teaching example).
- <https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1212914>

## Pillar intersections

### Nutrition × sleep — mechanistic crossover trials `sleepRestriction`
- **Design:** randomised crossover experiments + mechanistic meta-analysis of acute sleep restriction in healthy adults.
- **Finding:** sleep restriction lowered insulin sensitivity (~18.6% Matsuda), raised ghrelin, increased intake (~+328 kcal snacks) and sweet-taste preference; habitual short sleep links to higher obesity/diabetes risk.
- **Evidence level:** randomised crossover + mechanistic meta-analysis (moderate-to-high for short-term mechanism).
- <https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7071396/>

### Nutrition × stress — chronic stress & "comfort food" `dallman2003`
- **Design:** foundational mechanistic review (PNAS) + a small exploratory RCT of a mindfulness-based stress-eating intervention.
- **Finding:** chronic stress raises cortisol → energy-dense "comfort food" preference and abdominal-fat redistribution; mindfulness reduced cortisol & abdominal fat in overweight women.
- **Evidence level:** foundational review + **small exploratory RCT (moderate)**.
- **⚠ Teach with caveat:** the mindfulness result is **hypothesis-generating** (small, exploratory) — present as such.
- <https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1934666100>

### Nutrition × social connection — social-support weight loss `socialSupport`
- **Design:** 2024 systematic review & meta-analysis of social-support-based weight-loss interventions + a prospective cohort on support/undermining.
- **Finding:** social-support interventions help, especially longer-term; support from friends/coworkers/family predicts weight reduction, family undermining predicts weight gain.
- **Evidence level:** SR/meta-analysis + prospective cohort (moderate).
- <https://www.nature.com/articles/s41366-024-01468-9>

## Counselling models

### Motivational interviewing — review of reviews + training study `frost2018mi`
- **Finding:** MI improves adherence to dietary recommendations and outcomes (glycaemic control, weight); brief MI training changed dietitians' counselling style and lowered patients' saturated-fat intake → MI is a **learnable, transferable** skill.
- **Evidence level:** systematic reviews / reviews of reviews + controlled training study (moderate-to-high).
- <https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6193639/>

### Transtheoretical (Stages-of-Change) model — Cochrane review `mastellos2014ttm`
- **Finding:** increase in perceived "pros" outweighs decrease in "cons" toward action (justifies readiness-matched messaging); **limited evidence** that staging alone produces sustained weight loss.
- **Evidence level:** Cochrane SR (low-certainty for weight outcomes) + meta-analysis (mixed).
- **⚠ Teach with humility:** a useful heuristic, **not** a proven weight-loss intervention — a deliberate lesson in distinguishing heuristic from outcome evidence.
- <https://www.cochranelibrary.com/cdsr/doi/10.1002/14651858.CD008066.pub3/full>

## Education evidence

### Nutrition in medical education — Lancet Planetary Health SR `crowley2019`
- **Finding:** nutrition is consistently **insufficiently taught** worldwide; graduates feel least prepared in clinical nutrition, nutrition in primary care, and **evidence-based nutrition** — signalling where to deliberately strengthen this curriculum.
- **Evidence level:** systematic review + rapid review.
- <https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31538623/>
