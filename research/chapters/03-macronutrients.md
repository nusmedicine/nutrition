# Macronutrients: Carbohydrate, Protein, Fat and Fibre — research dossier

## 1. Scope (one paragraph)
This chapter introduces the three energy-yielding macronutrients (carbohydrate, protein, fat) plus dietary fibre, framed for first-year medical students through a nutrition-led, lifestyle-medicine lens and anchored Singapore-first. The emphasis is on *quality over quantity*: what each macronutrient is, its core physiological roles, how its different forms (e.g. free sugars vs whole grains; saturated vs unsaturated vs trans fat) relate to non-communicable disease (NCD) risk, and where the major guideline thresholds (WHO, HPB/MOH, AHA) sit. Students should leave able to answer the lay question "are carbs/fats bad?" with nuance, to read a population-level adequacy statistic (e.g. the Singapore NNS 2022 ~4% wholegrain-staples finding), and to connect macronutrient *patterns* to cardiometabolic outcomes. This is a foundational map of roles and thresholds — **not** a therapeutic prescribing chapter; clinical macronutrient titration, carbohydrate counting, and disease-specific medical nutrition therapy are signposted as later/specialist learning.

## 2. Learning objectives (refined)
By the end of this chapter, the first-year student can:
1. **Classify** the macronutrients (carbohydrate, protein, fat) and dietary fibre, state their approximate energy contributions (CHO/protein ~4 kcal/g, fat ~9 kcal/g, fibre ~2 kcal/g, alcohol ~7 kcal/g for contrast), and **summarise the core physiological role(s)** of each.
2. **Distinguish carbohydrate by quality** — free sugars vs intrinsic sugars, refined vs whole grains, and the concept (not the computation) of glycaemic index/load — and explain why "quality" matters more than a single "good/bad" label.
3. **Distinguish fat types** (saturated, *trans*, mono- and polyunsaturated, including omega-3/omega-6) and relate each to cardiovascular risk, recognising *trans* fat as the clearest harm signal.
4. **Explain the benefits of dietary fibre and whole grains using named evidence** (Reynolds 2019 *Lancet* dose-response meta-analyses), including the strength and limits of that evidence.
5. **Relate WHO/HPB macronutrient thresholds to disease risk** (free sugars <10% energy, saturated fat <10%, *trans* fat <1%, total fat <30%, salt <5 g/day) and interpret Singapore population adequacy data (NNS 2022) against them.
6. **Apply** this to a common patient/lay question ("are carbs bad?") at a counselling-appropriate, first-year level, knowing when to refer to a dietitian.

## 3. Scope boundaries
**In:**
- Definitions, energy yield, digestion/absorption *in outline*, and core roles of CHO, protein, fat, fibre.
- Carbohydrate quality: free/added sugars, intrinsic sugars, whole vs refined grains, fibre types (soluble/insoluble, fermentable), resistant starch (brief), GI/GL conceptually.
- Fat quality: saturated, *trans* (industrial vs ruminant), MUFA, PUFA (omega-3/-6), dietary cholesterol in brief.
- Protein: essential vs non-essential amino acids, protein quality (animal vs plant), adequacy concept; the RDA (~0.8 g/kg/day) as a population reference.
- Guideline thresholds (WHO, HPB My Healthy Plate, MOH/HPB Nutri-Grade, AHA 2021) and what they are based on.
- Singapore population adequacy (NNS 2022) and hawker-food worked examples.

**Out (covered elsewhere):**
- Micronutrients, vitamins, minerals, hydration → Chapter on micronutrients/hydration (AfN topic G).
- Energy balance, weight regulation, obesity classification (Asian BMI cut-offs) → energy balance / obesity chapter.
- Glycaemic control, diabetes screening and management, carbohydrate counting → diabetes chapter (`mohWarOnDiabetes`, `aceT2DM2023`).
- Lipid-lowering and therapeutic diets, CVD risk management → CVD/lipids chapter (`aceLipid2023`).
- Dietary *patterns* as interventions (DASH, Mediterranean) → dietary-patterns chapter (`appel1997dash`, `estruch2018predimed`).
- Therapeutic macronutrient prescription / clinical MNT → signposted to later years (`ada2024care`).

## 4. Key concepts to teach (didactic spine)
Ordered; each tied to evidence (BibTeX key in §5/§12).

1. **Macronutrients are the energy-yielding nutrients; quality, not just quantity, drives health.** Set the framing: same kcal, very different physiology. → `who2020diet`, `aha2021diet`
2. **Carbohydrate: a spectrum, not a single thing.** From free sugars → refined starches → whole grains/intact grains + fibre. Role: primary, glucose-yielding fuel; fibre as the "quality marker." → `who2020diet`, `reynolds2019carb`
3. **Free sugars are the carbohydrate to limit.** WHO defines free sugars (added + those in syrups/juices) and caps them at <10% (conditional further benefit below <5%) of energy. → `who2020diet`, `hpbNutriGrade`
4. **Whole grains and dietary fibre are protective.** Observational dose-response associations with lower all-cause/CV mortality, CHD, stroke, T2DM and colorectal cancer; associations strengthen up to ~25–29 g fibre/day. → `reynolds2019carb`
5. **Fat: type matters more than total.** Replacing saturated and (especially) *trans* fat with unsaturated fat is associated with lower CVD risk; total-fat ceilings are secondary to fat *quality*. → `who2020diet`, `aha2021diet`
6. ***Trans* fat is the clearest harm; cap <1% energy / eliminate industrial sources.** → `who2020diet`
7. **Protein: adequacy and quality.** Roles (structural, enzymatic, immune, transport); essential amino acids; animal vs plant protein; ~0.8 g/kg/day population reference; plant proteins bring fibre + unsaturated fat as a "package deal." → `who2020diet`, `aha2021diet`
8. **Guidelines increasingly emphasise *foods and patterns*, not isolated nutrients.** The "no single ideal macro split" message; minimise ultra-processed foods. → `aha2021diet`, `ada2024care`
9. **Singapore translates thresholds into practical tools.** My Healthy Plate (¼ wholegrains, ¼ protein, ½ fruit & veg) and Nutri-Grade beverage grading. → `hpbHealthyPlate`, `hpbNutriGrade`
10. **The local reality gap.** NNS 2022: ~4% of staples are wholegrain (vs ~30% recommended); saturated fat is a large share of fat intake; ~9/10 exceed the sodium target — motivates *why* the thresholds matter. → `mohNNS2022`
11. **Answering "are carbs/fats bad?"** Synthesise: the question is malformed; quality and food source determine the answer. Know the referral boundary to a dietitian. → `ada2024care`, `crowley2019`

## 6. Singapore context
- **My Healthy Plate (HPB, `hpbHealthyPlate`):** the primary first-year teaching anchor — ¼ plate wholegrains, ¼ protein, ½ fruit & vegetables, with "Quarter, Quarter, Half" mnemonic. Operationalises macronutrient *quality* into a visual most patients can act on.
- **Nutri-Grade (HPB/MOH, `hpbNutriGrade`):** mandatory front-of-pack grading for pre-packaged and freshly-prepared beverages (sugar/saturated-fat thresholds A–D). A live example of free-sugar policy translation; useful for teaching free sugars and behaviour-nudge design. Pair with the WHO free-sugar cap.
- **NNS 2022 (`mohNNS2022`) — the reality gap:** only **~4%** of staples consumed are wholegrain (against a ~30% recommendation); a large proportion of total fat intake is saturated (exceeding the <30%-of-fat / <10%-energy framing); and **~9 in 10** residents exceed the daily sodium target. These numbers make WHO/HPB thresholds concrete and motivate the chapter. *(Verify the exact percentages — saturated-fat share, mean sodium, total sugar — against the official HPB NNS 2022 report at write-time; figures here are sourced via the MOH newsroom release and should not be quoted to false precision.)*
- **Hawker-food worked examples (build into cases):**
  - White rice / *char kway teow* / *roti prata* → refined-carbohydrate, high-saturated-fat exemplars; contrast with brown-rice or wholemeal options.
  - *Teh tarik* / bubble tea / packaged drinks → free sugars + Nutri-Grade C/D; ideal Nutri-Grade teaching object.
  - *Chicken rice* → protein + fat (skin, oily rice) discussion; "ask for less oil/skin" as a quality nudge.
  - Wholegrain swaps available in SG (brown/red rice, wholemeal *prata*/bread) → make the ~4% statistic actionable.
- Honesty note: the Asian BMI cut-offs (`sgObesityBMI`) and War-on-Diabetes screening (`mohWarOnDiabetes`) belong to later chapters; reference only in passing.

## 7. Pillar intersections
- **Carbohydrate quality → glycaemic/metabolic health** (preview of diabetes chapter; `mohWarOnDiabetes`, `aceT2DM2023`). Fibre/whole grains and lower GI/GL foreshadow glycaemic control without prescribing here. If a remission/landmark trial is previewed at all, signpost honestly: DiRECT (`lean2018direct`) showed ~46% T2DM remission but only in an *early* (≤6 years), *non-insulin-treated*, BMI 27–45 population via an intensive weight-management programme — not a general claim that diabetes is reversible.
- **Fat quality → cardiovascular/lipid health** (preview of CVD/lipids; `aceLipid2023`, `aha2021diet`). Saturated/*trans* → unsaturated swap is the bridge.
- **Free sugars / Nutri-Grade → behaviour & food environment** (lifestyle-medicine pillar; policy nudges, `hpbNutriGrade`).
- **Macronutrient patterns → dietary-patterns chapter** (`appel1997dash`, `estruch2018predimed`) — patterns are the applied form of macronutrient quality. When previewing, caveat honestly: PREDIMED's ~30% figure is a *relative* risk reduction on a *composite* CV endpoint, the result is *stroke-dominated*, and the headline derives from the 2018 *re-analysis* after randomisation irregularities (`estruch2018predimed`); DASH lowers BP independent of weight/sodium change (`appel1997dash`).
- **Protein/fibre → satiety & energy balance** (preview of weight/obesity chapter). If a diabetes-prevention trial is mentioned, signpost that DPP's 58% risk reduction came from an *intensive 16-session lifestyle programme*, not a single clinic visit (`dpp2002`).
- **Nutrition literacy → clinical competency** (`crowley2019`, `aclm2022comp`, `afn2021curriculum`): foundational macronutrient fluency underpins all later applied nutrition.

## 8. Clinical application & case ideas (case-player-ready)

**Case A — "Doctor, are carbs bad? I cut out all rice."**
A 34-year-old office worker on a popular low-carb trend asks whether carbohydrates are harmful and reports eating mostly meat and eggs, skipping rice and bread.
- *Branch 1 — "Yes, cut all carbs":* → consequence: loses fibre, whole grains, plant foods; reveal fibre/whole-grain protective evidence (`reynolds2019carb`). Correct toward *quality*.
- *Branch 2 — "No, carbs are fine, eat as much white rice as you like":* → consequence: free-sugar/refined-starch overload; introduce GI/GL concept and NNS wholegrain gap (`mohNNS2022`).
- *Branch 3 (target) — "It depends on the type":* distinguish free sugars/refined vs whole grains + fibre; apply My Healthy Plate (`hpbHealthyPlate`); suggest brown-rice swap; flag dietitian referral if pursuing a restrictive diet (`ada2024care`). Reinforce: the question is malformed.

**Case B — Interpreting the NNS 2022 ~4% wholegrain finding (population/public-health reasoning).**
Student is shown the headline "only ~4% of staples consumed in Singapore are wholegrain (recommended ~30%)."
- *Branch 1:* "This means Singaporeans don't eat enough carbohydrate." → correct the misread: it is a *quality* gap, not a *quantity* gap.
- *Branch 2:* "Tell every patient to eat 30% wholegrain immediately." → nuance: behaviour change, food environment, hawker availability; Nutri-Grade-style nudges; gradual swaps.
- *Branch 3 (target):* link the statistic to the Reynolds fibre/whole-grain evidence (why it matters), to HPB tools (how to close it), and to the sodium/saturated-fat findings as a combined NCD-risk picture (`mohNNS2022`, `reynolds2019carb`, `who2020diet`). Discuss appropriate humility: this is correlational population data.

**Case C (stretch) — Reading a hawker meal.**
Deconstruct a *char kway teow + teh tarik* lunch into macronutrient quality (refined carb, saturated fat, free sugar, low fibre); propose realistic swaps; map the drink to Nutri-Grade.

## 9. Common misconceptions & pitfalls
- **"Carbs are bad" / "fat is bad."** Both are quality questions, not categorical bans — the single most important corrective in this chapter.
- **Confusing "sugar" terms:** free/added sugars vs total sugars vs intrinsic sugars (e.g. whole fruit). WHO targets *free* sugars; whole-fruit sugar is not the target.
- **Treating all fibre as one.** Soluble vs insoluble, fermentable vs not — different roles; teach the distinction lightly without overreach.
- **Equating "whole grain" with "multigrain"/"brown-coloured"** marketing — colour and grain count ≠ intact wholegrain content.
- **Over-reading observational fibre/whole-grain data as proof of causation.** Reynolds 2019 is largely prospective cohort + some RCT; certainty is *moderate at best* (and *low–very low* for GI/GL) — teach the caveat.
- **Believing there is one "ideal" macronutrient ratio.** Guidelines explicitly reject a single split (`ada2024care`); patterns/food sources matter more.
- **Total-fat fixation.** Chasing "low fat" can swap fat for refined carbs/sugar (worse). Type of fat > total fat.
- **Dietary cholesterol = blood cholesterol** (oversimplified; saturated/*trans* fat matter more) — flag briefly, defer detail.
- **Prescribing grams.** First-year overreach risk: students should give *quality* guidance and refer, not titrate macros.

## 10. Proposed interactive elements
- **Quiz topics:**
  1. Match macronutrient → energy yield (4/9/2 kcal/g) and one core role.
  2. Classify foods as free-sugar / intrinsic-sugar / whole-grain / refined.
  3. Rank fats by CVD risk (*trans* > saturated > MUFA/PUFA) and pick a healthy swap.
  4. Match WHO/HPB thresholds to numbers (free sugar <10%, sat fat <10%, *trans* <1%, fat <30%, salt <5 g).
  5. Nutri-Grade: assign A–D to beverages by g sugar/100 ml.
- **Diagram idea (textual concept):** "The carbohydrate quality spectrum" — a single horizontal ramp from *free sugars / refined → intact whole grains + fibre*, annotated with the Reynolds 2019 outcome associations at the high-fibre end and the My Healthy Plate ¼-wholegrain anchor. Companion mini-diagram: "fat quality ladder" (*trans* → saturated → MUFA → PUFA/omega-3) with the unsaturated-swap arrow.
- **Case:** Case A ("are carbs bad?") as the flagship branching case-player scenario, with Case B (NNS interpretation) as a public-health companion.
- **Worked local example widget:** a clickable hawker tray that decomposes a meal into macronutrient quality + Nutri-Grade.

## 11. Evidence gaps / open questions
- **Causality limits:** whole-grain/fibre benefits rest largely on observational cohorts; RCTs are shorter and surrogate-heavy. State the residual-confounding caveat (`reynolds2019carb`).
- **Saturated-fat nuance:** active scientific debate on saturated fat in the context of *food matrix* (e.g. dairy vs processed meat) and *replacement nutrient* — the "replace with what?" question. Keep the first-year message simple (replace with unsaturated/whole foods) but acknowledge the ongoing debate.
- **Optimal macro split:** no consensus "ideal" ratio; guidelines deliberately avoid one (`ada2024care`). Honest unknown.
- **GI/GL:** lowest-certainty carbohydrate-quality metric in Reynolds 2019; teach as a *concept*, not a clinical tool, at this level.
- **Local data currency:** confirm the precise NNS 2022 figures (saturated-fat share of fat, mean sodium, total sugar) against the official HPB report when consulting the full PDF; headline figures here are sourced via the MOH newsroom release (2022). Verify whether a newer survey supersedes NNS 2022 by write-time.
- **Citation verification (status):**
  - `reynolds2019carb` — **verified** against *The Lancet* (2019;393(10170):434–445; doi:10.1016/S0140-6736(18)31809-9).
  - `aha2021diet` — **verified**: Lichtenstein et al., *Circulation* 2021;144:e472–e487; doi:10.1161/CIR.0000000000001031. **Note:** the AHA issued an updated 2026 dietary-guidance statement (doi:10.1161/CIR.0000000000001435); decide at write-time whether to cite the 2021 statement, the 2026 update, or both.
