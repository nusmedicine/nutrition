## 1. Scope (one paragraph)

This chapter teaches first-year medical students to **recognise the dietary levers** that lower blood pressure and atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) risk, and to know **when to refer or co-manage** — not to prescribe the regimen. The Singapore-first anchor is that **cardiovascular disease is the nation's leading killer (~1 in 3 deaths)** and **~1 in 3 adults has hypertension, more than half of them undiagnosed** — so nutrition here is population-scale primary prevention, delivered lifestyle-first. The didactic spine runs: (1) think **whole dietary patterns, not single nutrients** (AHA 2021 / WHO); (2) the **DASH pattern** lowers blood pressure in its own right, and stacks additively with sodium reduction; (3) **sodium down, potassium up** is a genuine, graded, reversible BP lever — with hard-outcome support from salt-substitute trials and an honestly-taught J-curve controversy; (4) **fat quality beats fat quantity** — swap saturated/trans for unsaturated to lower LDL-C and events, and always ask "replaced with *what*?"; (5) the **Mediterranean pattern** lowered cardiovascular *events* in PREDIMED (stroke-dominated, ~30% *relative*, in a high-risk Spanish cohort, republished after retraction) — teach the principles, not the effect size; (6) **fish the food ≠ omega-3 the capsule ≠ icosapent ethyl the drug**; (7) **alcohol is not cardioprotective** — the J-curve is confounding, and cutting intake is a real BP/triglyceride lever; (8) all of this is **operationalised through My Healthy Plate and hawker swaps**, with lipid/BP drug targets **signposted** to the ACE guidances and SG-FRS-2023, never drilled. The graduate can explain *why* a heart-healthy pattern works, translate it to a hawker stall, and recognise the referral/co-management trigger.

## 2. Learning objectives

By the end of the chapter, a student can:

1. **Explain** why cardiovascular-diet advice is framed around **whole dietary patterns rather than single nutrients**, using the AHA 2021 pattern features and WHO population targets as the framework. `[aha2021diet, who2020diet]`
2. **Describe** the **DASH pattern** and **explain** how it lowers blood pressure (potassium/magnesium/fibre + whole-food matrix), that it is **additive with sodium reduction**, and — honestly — that the landmark evidence is short controlled-feeding with a **surrogate BP endpoint**, with a smaller free-living effect. `[appel1997dash, sacks2001dashsodium, alsolaiman2010dashmechanism, filippou2020dashmeta, saneei2014dashmeta, appel2003premier]`
3. **Outline** the **sodium–blood-pressure relationship** (graded, dose-dependent, larger in hypertensives), the **counter-regulatory role of potassium**, the population target (WHO salt <5 g/day), and **recognise** the salt-substitute hard-outcome evidence *and* its renal caveat (signposted to the Chronic Kidney Disease chapter). `[who-sodium-factsheet, aha-sodium-target, he2013saltbp, huang2020sodiumdose, who2012potassium, neal2021ssass, cappuccio2022sodiummyths]`
4. **Explain** that dietary-fat effects on blood lipids are about **quality and replacement** (saturated/trans → unsaturated lowers LDL-C and events; "replaced with what?" resolves the saturated-fat controversy), and **distinguish** oily fish (a recommended food) from omega-3 **supplements** (failed in prevention) and from **icosapent ethyl** (a drug — signpost only). `[mensink2003, who2018transfat, hooper2020, sacks2017, siritarino2010, chowdhury2014, manson2019vital, ascend2018omega3, bhatt2019reduceit]`
5. **Describe** the **Mediterranean pattern** as an event-lowering, stroke-dominated, **relative**-risk signal from PREDIMED — WITH the retraction→republication story and the generalisability caveat — and **recognise** guideline endorsement of pattern-level eating (AHA, ESC). `[estruch2018predimed, predimedHR2018, predimedRetractionHSPH, medUmbrella, salasSalvado2019predimedplus, esc2021prevention, aha2021diet]`
6. **Explain** why **alcohol is not cardioprotective** (J-curve = confounding + sick-quitter bias, undercut by Mendelian-randomisation), and **recognise** alcohol reduction as a reversible BP/triglyceride lever and Singapore's low-risk drinking framing. `[anderson2023nosafe, who2023nolevelsafe, millwood2019alcohol, biddinger2022alcohol, gbd2018alcohol, zhao2023allcause, roerecke2017bp, klop2013alcoholtg, hpb2023alcohol, moh2021nphsbinge]`
7. **Recall** the Singapore epidemiology, **recognise** the ACE Hypertension and Lipid guidances and the recalibrated **SG-FRS-2023** (a *coronary*-risk tool, not an all-ASCVD score), and **recognise** where lifestyle counselling ends and **dietitian referral / guideline-directed co-management** begins — WITHOUT memorising drug thresholds. `[shfHeartStats2024, nhcsBurdenCvd, nphs2024, nphs2023hh, aceHtn2023, aceLipid2023, sgfrs2023, ada2026care, mohEatWiseSG, hsgCareProtocols2023]`
8. **Recognise** the ultra-processed-food lever honestly — a consistent, dose-dependent, but **observational and low-certainty** association with CVD. `[lane2024upf, qu2024upfcvd]`

*(Verb level intentionally capped at recognise/classify/describe/explain/outline — no "manage with drug X" objectives at first year.)*

## 4. Key concepts to teach (didactic spine)

1. **CVD is Singapore's biggest, mostly preventable killer, and hypertension is silent and half-undiagnosed.** ~30–31% of all deaths are cardiovascular (30.5% in 2024, ~22/day; ~31% in 2022); measured hypertension is **~34% of adults 18–74 with 51% undiagnosed and 60% of known cases poorly controlled** (NPHS 2024). This is the population-scale rationale that opens the chapter. `[shfHeartStats2024, nhcsBurdenCvd, nphs2024]`
2. **Think patterns, not single nutrients.** The AHA 2021 statement organises heart-healthy eating into ~10 *pattern* features "beyond individual foods or nutrients", and WHO gives a small set of population targets (salt <5 g/day; saturated fat <10%E; trans <1%E; free sugars <10%E; ≥400 g fruit/veg). This is the umbrella that ties sodium, fat, DASH and Mediterranean together. `[aha2021diet, who2020diet]`
3. **DASH lowers blood pressure — proven by controlled feeding.** The fruit/veg/low-fat-dairy/wholegrain pattern reduced BP **~5.5/3.0 mmHg overall and ~11.4/5.5 mmHg in hypertensives**, with sodium and weight held constant — so the food *pattern itself* did the work. **Caveat:** 8-week feeding study, surrogate (office BP) endpoint; free-living effect is smaller. `[appel1997dash]`
4. **DASH and sodium reduction are additive.** DASH-Sodium: cutting sodium helps on *both* diets, DASH helps at *every* sodium level, and **DASH-at-low-sodium is lowest of all** (~11.5 mmHg lower systolic in hypertensives vs control-at-high-sodium). Two levers, pull both. `[sacks2001dashsodium]`
5. **DASH is a *pattern*, working through potassium/magnesium/fibre + the whole-food matrix — not a single nutrient.** The whole diet beats the matched supplements; steer to potassium-rich produce, not pills. WHO independently backs higher dietary potassium. `[alsolaiman2010dashmechanism, who2012potassium]`
6. **The honest pooled DASH effect is ~3–7/2–4 mmHg — modest but population-meaningful, bigger in hypertensives and at high baseline sodium.** Use meta-analytic numbers to avoid both nihilism and over-promising; expect the *smaller* real-world (free-living, PREMIER) effect. `[filippou2020dashmeta, saneei2014dashmeta, appel2003premier]`
7. **Sodium → BP is graded and dose-dependent; potassium is the counter-regulator.** Sodium raises volume/pressure; potassium promotes natriuresis and vasodilation. Population target: WHO salt <5 g/day (AHA stricter, tiered to 1500 mg). `[who-sodium-factsheet, aha-sodium-target, he2013saltbp, huang2020sodiumdose, who2012potassium]`
8. **Salt substitution has HARD-outcome evidence — with a renal caveat to signpost.** A 25%-KCl substitute cut stroke, major CV events and death (SSaSS) — but the trial *excluded* serious kidney disease and potassium-drug users. Renal K⁺ safety → the Chronic Kidney Disease chapter. `[neal2021ssass]`
9. **The sodium J-curve is largely a measurement artefact — and irrelevant at Singapore's high intake.** Contested only at the extreme-low end; rigorous 24-h-urine cohorts show a linear relationship. At a mean ~3,600 mg/day, essentially everyone benefits from cutting down. `[cappuccio2022sodiummyths]`
10. **Fat quality, not quantity.** Replacing saturated (and especially trans) fat with cis-unsaturated fat lowers the atherogenic total:HDL ratio and LDL-C; this is the actionable *swap*, not "eat less fat". `[mensink2003, who2018transfat]`
11. **Reducing saturated fat cuts events — and the "SFA is fine" controversy resolves once you ask 'replaced with what?'.** Cochrane: **−17% combined CV events** (RR 0.83; moderate certainty). AHA: SFA→PUFA swap ~**−30%**, statin-like. The null cohort meta-analyses (Siri-Tarino, Chowdhury) didn't specify the replacement; PUFA protects, refined carbs/sugar do not. `[hooper2020, sacks2017, siritarino2010, chowdhury2014]`
12. **Fish ≠ supplement ≠ drug.** Eat oily fish (whole food); routine omega-3 **capsules failed** in primary prevention (VITAL, ASCEND); **icosapent ethyl** (REDUCE-IT) is a high-dose *prescription drug* — SIGNPOST only, do not extrapolate to fish oil. `[manson2019vital, ascend2018omega3, bhatt2019reduceit]`
13. **The Mediterranean pattern lowered cardiovascular *events*, stroke-dominated, ~30% *relative*, taught WITH the retraction story.** PREDIMED (high-CV-risk Spain): composite HR ~0.69/0.72; only *stroke* individually significant; original 2013 paper retracted for randomisation irregularities and **republished 2018** with the conclusion upheld. Teach principles, not the effect size. `[estruch2018predimed, predimedHR2018, predimedRetractionHSPH]`
14. **Wider Mediterranean evidence is consistent but low-certainty; PREDIMED-Plus is intermediate-outcome so far.** Umbrella review: protective direction but 84% of reviews low/critically-low quality. PREDIMED-Plus (energy-restricted MedDiet + activity) has moved risk factors, not yet hard events. Guidelines (AHA, ESC) endorse the *pattern*. `[medUmbrella, salasSalvado2019predimedplus, esc2021prevention, aha2021diet]`
15. **Alcohol is not cardioprotective; the J-curve is confounding.** WHO "no safe level"; Mendelian-randomisation (China Kadoorie, UK Biobank) shows continuous risk with no protective threshold; GBD "zero minimises harm"; sick-quitter bias explains the apparent benefit. Reducing alcohol lowers BP (dose-dependently, most in heavy drinkers) and triglycerides — **never a reason to start**. `[anderson2023nosafe, who2023nolevelsafe, millwood2019alcohol, biddinger2022alcohol, gbd2018alcohol, zhao2023allcause, roerecke2017bp, klop2013alcoholtg, hpb2023alcohol]`
16. **Ultra-processed food is a real but low-certainty lever.** Consistent, dose-dependent higher CVD risk in large observational syntheses (RR ~1.50 for CVD mortality; ~+1.9% CV events per +10% UPF) — but observational, GRADE very-low, mechanism-beyond-nutrients unresolved. `[lane2024upf, qu2024upfcvd]`
17. **Risk is *estimated*, and the tool must fit the population.** Singapore recalibrated Framingham/NCEP-ATP-III into **SG-FRS-2023** (local MEC1 + NRDO); it estimates 10-year **coronary** (non-fatal MI / coronary death) risk — **not** stroke or total ASCVD. Teach *why* recalibration exists, not the score sheet. `[sgfrs2023]`
18. **Lifestyle first, then refer/co-manage — drug targets are SIGNPOSTED, not recalled.** ACE Hypertension and Lipid guidances are risk-tiered and lifestyle-first; a shared SG-FRS-2023 calculator generates LDL and BP targets by risk; refer to a dietitian (EatWise SG) for structured change; escalate pharmacotherapy per risk tier. First-years know the guidance *exists and is risk-tiered*, not the thresholds. `[aceHtn2023, aceLipid2023, ada2026care, mohEatWiseSG, hsgCareProtocols2023]`

## 6. Singapore context

- **Open with the burden.** CVD is the **leading cause of death** — 30.5% of deaths in 2024 (~22 people/day; `shfHeartStats2024`), ~31% in 2022 (`nhcsBurdenCvd`). Teach it as "about a third of deaths, most of it modifiable." **Hypertension is silent and half-undiagnosed:** NPHS 2024 measured prevalence **33.8%** of adults 18–74, **51.2% undiagnosed**, and **60.4% of known hypertensives poorly controlled** (`nphs2024`). *(Refresh from the earlier dossier's 37%/53% NPHS-2022 figures — the story is unchanged; the numbers are updated.)* Note the **self-report vs measured** trap: NPHS 2023 (Household Interview) reports only **15.0%** self-reported HTN — an acknowledged under-count — while the "true" ~34% comes from the measured Health Examination (`nphs2023hh`, `nphs2024`). Great teaching hook for *why measured screening matters for a silent disease*.
- **Sodium is the headline dietary problem.** NNS 2022: **~9 in 10 residents exceed the 2000 mg/day sodium limit**, averaging **~3,600 mg/day (~2× WHO)**, most of it from **added salt, sauces/seasonings and food eaten outside the home** (`hpb2023sodium`). Hawker soups/gravies/condiments hide the salt: fishball noodle soup ~2,913 mg (~145% of the daily limit), lor mee ~2,538 mg, ban mian ~2,196 mg (Singapore Heart Foundation; described qualitatively here for laksa/BKT as "soup/sauce-heavy" — no primary mg value confirmed). Behavioural levers to *recognise and advise* (not prescribe): **leave most of the soup, sauce/gravy on the side, ask for "less salt", skip added soy/chilli, add vegetables/fruit for potassium.**
- **National salt-reduction machinery to recognise.** HPB's **15%-by-2026** sodium target, the reduced-sodium ingredient subsidy (lower-sodium salt/sauces now price-comparable so hawkers can switch), the industry **Sodium Reduction Pledge**, and the **HCS "Lower in Sodium"** label (≥25% less sodium than comparable products; `hpbHcsSodium`, `hpb2023sodium`). From **mid-2027**, **Nutri-Grade extends beyond beverages to salt, sauces, seasonings, instant noodles and cooking oils (SSSIO)** — closing the "beverage-only" gap (`moh2025nutrigrade`).
- **The local fat environment.** Saturated fat is **36% of total fat consumed** (>30% recommendation), with **cooking oil the single largest source** (`moh2025nutrigrade`). Everyday SFA sources to recognise: coconut milk/*santan* (nasi lemak, curries), ghee, palm-oil deep-frying, reused frying oil (you tiao, fried bee hoon). The lever is the *type of oil* at home and at the stall.
- **My Healthy Plate = DASH in Singapore's visual language.** Half fruit/veg, quarter wholegrains, quarter protein (`hpbHealthyPlate`) — the ready-to-prescribe local operationalisation of DASH/Mediterranean principles. Translate to hawker reality: sliced-fish soup with brown rice and extra vegetables; **yong tau foo** (clear soup, more vegetables/tofu, fewer fried items, *not* laksa gravy); steamed over deep-fried fish; **teh-O kosong** over sweet drinks.
- **The Singapore risk-tool contrast.** Singapore uses **SG-FRS-2023**, recalibrated from Framingham/NCEP-ATP-III using the local **Multi-Ethnic Cohort (MEC1)** + **NRDO**, because imported US equations (Framingham, pooled-cohort) **mis-estimate risk across Chinese/Malay/Indian groups**. Crucial nuance to state plainly: SG-FRS-2023 estimates **10-year coronary** (non-fatal MI / coronary death) risk — it does **NOT** predict ischaemic stroke or total ASCVD (`sgfrs2023`). Teach *why* population-specific recalibration exists, not the score sheet.
- **The national guidances to signpost (not drill).** Singapore has a dedicated **ACE Hypertension** guidance (15 Dec 2023): HTN at clinic BP ≥140/90; risk assessed via SG-FRS-2023; risk-tiered BP targets; and — directly reinforcing this chapter — it names **sodium and alcohol reduction** (plus activity, weight, smoking cessation) as **first-line lifestyle intervention for all BP patients**, signposting an HPB "High blood pressure: healthy eating guide" (`aceHtn2023`). Its companion **ACE Lipid** guidance (15 Dec 2023; v1.1 26 Jun 2025) is risk-tiered, lifestyle-foundational, with statins first-line, ezetimibe add-on, and PCSK9/inclisiran reserved — **SIGNPOSTED, not prescribed** (`aceLipid2023`). NPHS 2024 even adopts the "**risk at every level**" alcohol framing locally, giving a Singapore government source for the alcohol-BP link (`nphs2024`).
- **The referral pathway is named locally.** The ADA "no single ideal macro split / refer for individualised MNT" principle (`ada2026care`) is operationalised by **EatWise SG** — a tiered Healthier SG model where GPs/nurses give first-line dietary advice and escalate complex cases (metabolic disease, malnutrition, frailty) to a registered dietitian (`mohEatWiseSG`) — alongside the 2023 **Healthier SG Care Protocols** for hypertension and lipid disorders (`hsgCareProtocols2023`).

## 7. Pillar intersections

- **nutrition × substances (alcohol).** Alcohol raises BP dose-dependently and elevates triglycerides; the "moderate alcohol is cardioprotective" claim is confounded and increasingly undercut by Mendelian-randomisation (`millwood2019alcohol`, `biddinger2022alcohol`). Frame as *not a reason to start or increase drinking*, and a genuine reversible lever in raised BP/lipids (`roerecke2017bp`, `klop2013alcoholtg`). Deeper substance-use content lives with the substances pillar; here it is a cardiovascular *diet-adjacent* lever.
- **nutrition × physical activity.** Aerobic activity independently lowers BP and improves lipids; the DASH/Mediterranean/PREMIER evidence is delivered as *bundles* (diet + activity + weight), not diet in isolation. Reinforce that dietary advice rides alongside activity (`appel2003premier`, `salasSalvado2019predimedplus`) — but keep weight-management mechanisms in ***the Obesity & Metabolic Syndrome chapter***.
- **nutrition × sleep/stress (light touch).** Poor sleep and chronic stress bias intake toward energy-dense, salty, ultra-processed foods that work against the pattern; treat as background modifiers here and route the mechanisms to their home chapters.

## 8. Clinical application & case ideas

*(In-chapter cases are **MCQ/choice-only** — the live AI patient-chat belongs only to the Integrated Cases chapter. Log in `../cases/case-bank.md`.)*

**Case A — "Mr Tan, 52, hawker-lunch regular, raised BP + LDL."** *(flagship branching MCQ case)*
- *Stem:* Chinese office worker, most meals at the hawker centre. Repeat clinic BP 148/92 then 146/90. Non-fasting lipids: raised LDL-C. No prior CVD, non-smoker, no diabetes. BMI 27.3. Kopi-C ×2/day, fishball noodle *soup* most days, <5,000 steps. Asks: "Do I need medication, doctor?"
- *Branch 1 — First move:* ✔ *Confirm BP over repeated readings, take a diet/activity/alcohol history, start lifestyle-first counselling, and arrange formal risk assessment.* / ✘ *Immediately start a statin + antihypertensive* → feedback: at recognise level the frame is **lifestyle-first + risk estimation before treatment**; drug thresholds are risk-tiered and looked up in the ACE guidances (`aceHtn2023`, `aceLipid2023`) — a clinician decision, not memorised here. / ✘ *Reassure, review in a year* → misses a modifiable, high-prevalence window.
- *Branch 2 — Which dietary advice?* ✔ *DASH-aligned pattern mapped onto My Healthy Plate + sodium reduction* → DASH is the BP-specific evidence (feeding-study effect; expect smaller free-living gains — `appel1997dash`, `appel2003premier`); pairing with sodium reduction is additive (`sacks2001dashsodium`). / ✘ *"Cut all fat"* → it's fat *quality/replacement* (saturated→unsaturated), not blanket avoidance (`mensink2003`, `sacks2017`). / ✘ *"Just stop adding table salt"* → most sodium is hidden in soup-bases/sauces/eaten-out food (`hpb2023sodium`).
- *Branch 3 — Hawker translation (select-all):* reward *leave most of the soup, sauce on the side, brown rice, yong tau foo with more vegetables, steamed over fried, teh-O kosong, add fruit* over generic "eat healthy."
- *Branch 4 — When to refer/co-manage:* ✔ *Refer to a dietitian (EatWise SG) for structured pattern change + arrange SG-FRS-2023 risk assessment and re-check BP/lipids; co-manage per ACE guidance by risk tier* (`mohEatWiseSG`, `sgfrs2023`, `aceLipid2023`). **Mandatory teaching pop-up:** *"You do NOT memorise LDL-C or BP targets — know they are risk-tiered and looked up in the ACE guidance; a shared SG-FRS-2023 calculator generates them. Applying them is clinician detail."*

**Case B — "Auntie Devi, 58, salt-substitute question."** *(do-no-harm / renal signpost)*
- *Stem:* Hypertensive, wants to switch to a "heart-healthy" potassium salt substitute she saw online.
- *Branch — Advice:* ✔ *For most people a 25%-KCl substitute is a reasonable sodium-cutting lever with hard-outcome support (SSaSS), but first check she has no kidney disease and isn't on a potassium-sparing diuretic / ACE inhibitor / ARB / potassium supplement — if so, do not recommend it and involve her doctor* (`neal2021ssass`). / ✘ *"Potassium salt is always safe, use freely"* → feedback: SSaSS **excluded** serious CKD and potassium-drug users; renal K⁺ safety is owned by ***the Chronic Kidney Disease chapter***. Trains the recognise-the-caveat-and-refer reflex.

**Case C — "Mr Lim, 40, 'a beer a day is good for my heart'."** *(myth correction)*
- *Branch — Respond:* ✔ *Gently correct: current evidence finds no safe level and the apparent 'protection' is confounding; cutting down lowers BP and triglycerides — but never advise a non-drinker to start* (`who2023nolevelsafe`, `millwood2019alcohol`, `roerecke2017bp`). Quantify his intake in **standard drinks** (10 g each; men ≤2/day — a harm-reduction ceiling, not a safe target; `hpb2023alcohol`). Screen for **session-based binge** patterns, not just weekly units (`moh2021nphsbinge`).

**Case D — "Fish, capsule, or drug?"** *(single-best-answer)*
- *Stem:* Patient asks if he should buy fish-oil capsules "for his heart." Best answer distinguishes: *eat oily fish (a whole food)* ✔; *routine omega-3 supplements did not prevent events (VITAL, ASCEND)* ✘-as-prevention; *high-dose icosapent ethyl is a prescription drug, not a supplement, and a specialist decision* (signpost) (`manson2019vital`, `ascend2018omega3`, `bhatt2019reduceit`).

## 9. Common misconceptions & pitfalls

1. **"Salt is the only thing that matters for blood pressure."** → Sodium is one lever; the *whole DASH pattern* (potassium, fibre, low-fat dairy, fruit/veg) lowered BP *before* sodium was changed, and the whole matrix beats the matched supplements (`appel1997dash`, `alsolaiman2010dashmechanism`). Best results combine pattern + sodium reduction.
2. **"Cutting salt does nothing / too little to bother."** → Modest reduction gives a graded, dose-dependent BP fall, larger in hypertensives, and salt substitution cut *hard outcomes* (`he2013saltbp`, `huang2020sodiumdose`, `neal2021ssass`). Modest ≠ meaningless at population scale.
3. **"The sodium J-curve means low salt is dangerous, so don't cut."** → The low-end uptick is largely a **measurement/reverse-causation artefact** (single spot-urine formulas, sick people eating less); rigorous 24-h-urine cohorts are linear, and at Singapore's ~3,600 mg/day mean essentially everyone is well above any putative threshold (`cappuccio2022sodiummyths`).
4. **"Low-fat = heart-healthy."** → What protects is *replacing saturated/trans with unsaturated* fat; "low-fat" swapped for refined starch/sugar is not protective — always ask **"replaced with what?"** (`mensink2003`, `sacks2017`).
5. **"Saturated fat has been exonerated."** → The null cohort meta-analyses didn't specify the replacement; RCT/substitution evidence shows SFA→PUFA cuts events ~30% and Cochrane shows −17% (`siritarino2010`, `chowdhury2014`, `sacks2017`, `hooper2020`). The controversy resolves at the replacement, not in favour of butter.
6. **"Fish-oil capsules protect the heart."** → Routine omega-3 **supplements** failed in primary prevention (VITAL, ASCEND); recommend the **fish**, not the capsule. Do NOT extrapolate from **icosapent ethyl** — a high-dose *drug* in a different population (`manson2019vital`, `ascend2018omega3`, `bhatt2019reduceit`).
7. **"PREDIMED was retracted, so the Mediterranean diet is debunked."** → It was retracted *and republished* (2018) after randomisation irregularities; the composite benefit held, driven by **stroke** (`estruch2018predimed`, `predimedRetractionHSPH`). Teach as science self-correcting.
8. **"A Mediterranean diet definitely slashes heart attacks by 30%."** → It was a ~30% ***relative*** reduction on a **composite** endpoint, **stroke-dominated, no significant MI-alone effect**, in a **high-risk Spanish** cohort — not transportable wholesale. Teach the principles, respect relative-vs-absolute (`estruch2018predimed`, `predimedHR2018`).
9. **"A bit of red wine is good for the heart, so encourage it."** → Confounded, sick-quitter-biased, undercut by Mendelian randomisation; alcohol raises BP and triglycerides. Never a reason to start or increase (`who2023nolevelsafe`, `biddinger2022alcohol`, `roerecke2017bp`).
10. **"I must memorise the LDL-C / BP target."** → No. Targets are **risk-tiered and looked up** in the ACE guidances (a shared SG-FRS-2023 calculator generates them) — awareness, not recall (`aceLipid2023`, `aceHtn2023`, `sgfrs2023`).
11. **"Use the American risk calculator."** → US Framingham/pooled-cohort equations mis-estimate Asian risk; Singapore uses the recalibrated **SG-FRS-2023** — and even that is a **coronary**-risk tool, *not* an all-ASCVD or stroke score (`sgfrs2023`).
12. **"Heart-healthy eating means Western/Mediterranean food only."** → The *principles* map onto hawker choices (fish soup, yong tau foo, brown rice, groundnuts, plant-oil cooking, fruit) via My Healthy Plate — culturally translate, don't import (`aha2021diet`, `hpbHealthyPlate`).
13. **"Ultra-processed food is a proven cardiovascular cause."** → The association is consistent and dose-dependent but **observational, GRADE very-low**, with mechanism-beyond-nutrients unresolved — a "minimise" lever, honestly framed (`lane2024upf`, `qu2024upfcvd`).

## 10. Proposed interactive elements

- **Quiz topics (MCQ):**
  1. Match pattern → primary evidence: **DASH = blood pressure / feeding RCT**; **Mediterranean = CV events / PREDIMED**.

  3. **PREDIMED caveat MCQ:** what does "retracted and republished" mean for how we cite it, and which endpoint actually drove the benefit (stroke)?

  5. **Fish vs capsule vs drug** single-best-answer (food recommended; supplement failed; icosapent ethyl = signposted drug).

  8. **Alcohol myth MCQ:** why is "moderate drinking protects the heart" no longer accepted (confounding / sick-quitter / MR)?
