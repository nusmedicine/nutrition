## 1. Scope (one paragraph)

This chapter teaches first-year medical students to **recognise the nutritional levers** in type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) — dysglycaemia on an insulin-resistance background — and to **refer** for the detailed regimen, rather than to prescribe it. It runs the length of the T2D continuum: **prevention** in people with prediabetes (the Diabetes Prevention Program, DPP, and its Singapore counterpart Pre-DICTED); everyday **dietary management** (carbohydrate *quantity and quality*, glycaemic index/load, fibre and wholegrains, sugar-sweetened beverages, and the acceptable dietary *patterns* — Mediterranean, low-carbohydrate, DASH-style, and the practical **plate method**); and **weight-centred remission** of *early* disease (DiRECT and supervised total diet replacement), taught with strict eligibility caveats and durability honesty. A safety spine runs throughout: **hypoglycaemia awareness** and **carbohydrate consistency** in counselling, because who is at hypo risk depends on drug class (insulin, sulfonylureas, meglitinides) — a first-year must recognise this *before* endorsing any carbohydrate-cutting diet. The whole chapter is anchored to Singapore's **War on Diabetes**, HPB/MOH tools (My Healthy Plate, Nutri-Grade), the local ethnic-risk gradient, and the culturally important, SG-relevant scenario of **Ramadan fasting with diabetes**. Medical nutrition therapy is **dietitian-led**; glucose-lowering pharmacology is **signposted** (metformin first-line; SGLT2 inhibitors / GLP-1 receptor agonists for cardiorenal risk per local ACE guidance), never taught in depth.

## 2. Learning objectives

By the end of the chapter, a first-year student can:

1. **Explain** that carbohydrate is the dietary component that most directly raises postprandial glucose, that there is **no single mandated carbohydrate target or "ideal" macronutrient ratio** for people with T2D, and that carbohydrate **quality plus day-to-day consistency** — individualised by a dietitian — matter more than any fixed gram prescription.
2. **Describe** the acceptable dietary **patterns** for T2D (Mediterranean, low-/very-low-carbohydrate, DASH-style, plant-based) and the **plate method** (HPB My Healthy Plate), and **outline** their T2D-specific evidence and honest limits — recognising the pattern to recommend, then referring for individualised MNT.
3. **Explain** that *early* T2D can be put into **remission** with substantial, sustained dietary weight loss (DiRECT; twin-cycle / personal-fat-threshold mechanism), and **state** the eligibility limits (≤6 years, non-insulin, a defined BMI band) and durability caveat (remission fades over time; remission ≠ cure).
4. **Summarise** the evidence that **structured** lifestyle change prevents progression from prediabetes to T2DM (DPP, Pre-DICTED), **outline** guideline-level prediabetes advice (~5–7% weight loss, ≥150 min/week activity, a quality pattern, referral to a structured programme), and **explain** why counselling should motivate without over-alarming.
5. **Recognise** which patients are at **hypoglycaemia** risk (insulin, sulfonylureas, meglitinides — NOT metformin/DPP-4/SGLT2/GLP-1 used alone), **describe** the "15-15" treatment of a conscious hypo and the principle of **carbohydrate consistency** for fixed-insulin regimens, and **refer** to the diabetes team before endorsing aggressive carbohydrate restriction or fasting.
6. **Outline** the **Ramadan-fasting** diabetes scenario (risk, pre-Ramadan risk-stratification and referral, the religious exemption for the sick) at recognise-and-refer altitude, and **explain** why it is a Singapore-first counselling-safety task.
7. **Situate** all of the above in Singapore's **War on Diabetes** — current epidemiology, the ethnic-risk gradient, HPB/MOH policy levers, and the local ACE guidance that **signposts** (not teaches) drug classes.

*(Verb level intentionally capped at recognise/classify/describe/explain/outline — no "manage with drug X", no gram targets, no insulin titration at first year.)*

## 4. Key concepts to teach (didactic spine)

The ordered concepts that become the chapter's sections — each tied to evidence in §5.

1. **T2D is dysglycaemia on an insulin-resistance background, and nutrition acts at every stage** of the continuum (at-risk → prediabetes → early T2D → established T2D → complications). The upstream obesity/insulin-resistance mechanism belongs to *the Obesity & Metabolic Syndrome chapter*; this chapter starts where dysglycaemia is established or imminent. *(Framing; ties `mohWarOnDiabetes`, `ada2026standards`.)*
2. **Carbohydrate is the key glycaemic driver — but there is NO ideal carbohydrate target or macronutrient ratio.** Quality plus day-to-day consistency, individualised by a dietitian, beats any fixed prescription. Recognise the lever; refer for MNT. *(`ada2026standards`, `dyson2018diabetesuk`, `evert2019nutrition`.)*
3. **Carbohydrate QUALITY: lower-GI/GL, wholegrain, higher-fibre choices give a small but real HbA1c benefit (~0.3% absolute)** — worth recommending, modest in size, and delivered as concrete swaps (brown rice, wholemeal bread, lower-Nutri-Grade drinks). *(`chiavaroli2021gigl`, `dnsg2023easd`.)*
4. **The glycaemic-index concept has honest limits:** GI is measured for single foods eaten alone; mixed meals (fat/protein/fibre) blunt the rise; inter-individual variability is large. Prefer wholegrain/high-fibre choices over memorising GI tables. *(`chiavaroli2021gigl`, `dnsg2023easd`.)*
5. **Fibre and wholegrains are the strongest, most consistent carbohydrate-quality signal** — higher intake lowers T2D incidence (16–24%) and, in established diabetes, lowers HbA1c and mortality; optimum ~25–29 g fibre/day. Singapore's biggest actionable gap (only ~4% of staples wholegrain). *(`reynolds2019carbquality`, `reynolds2020fibrediabetes`, `mohNNS2022`.)*
6. **Sugar-sweetened beverages are a discrete, high-yield lever:** each daily serving ≈ +13–18% T2D risk, partly weight-independent; the local hook is Nutri-Grade and the kopi/teh "kosong/siu dai" swap. *(`imamura2015ssb`, `shin2023nutrigrade`, `mohNNS2022`.)*
7. **Singapore honesty caveat — it's substitution, not demonising rice:** pooled Asian data link white rice to T2D, but the Singapore Chinese Health Study found rice intake itself *not* associated — the win is swapping *toward* wholegrain, not away from rice per se. *(`hu2012whiterice`, `seah2019scriphealthrice`.)*
8. **Guideline convergence:** ADA, Diabetes UK, EASD/DNSG and WHO agree — carbohydrate mainly from wholegrains/vegetables/fruit/legumes, minimise refined grains and SSBs, free sugars <10% energy. The consistency is itself the teaching point. *(`who2023carbohydrate`, `dnsg2023easd`.)*
9. **No single "ideal" diabetes diet — several PATTERNS are acceptable; quality beats the ratio.** Individualise to preference and culture; refer to a dietitian. *(`evert2019nutrition`, `dyson2018diabetesuk`.)*
10. **Mediterranean pattern has the deepest T2D-specific evidence** (HbA1c ~−0.47% in established T2D; ~20–23% lower incidence) — taught as the exemplar, translated locally via the plate method and swaps rather than adopted wholesale. **PREDIMED caveat:** it is a *CVD-prevention* trial, retracted/republished (2018); do NOT cite it for glycaemic control — CVD-risk diet is owned by *the Cardiovascular Disease & Hypertension chapter*. *(`esposito2015medjourney`, `koloverou2014medincidence`, `estruch2018predimed`.)*
11. **Low-/very-low-carbohydrate is an OPTION, not a mandate:** real ~6-month HbA1c/weight benefit that attenuates by 12 months; adherence is the limiting factor; strict keto is dietitian-supervised. *(`goldenberg2021lowcarb`.)*
12. **DASH-style eating improves glycaemic and BP measures** — but its named home is *the Cardiovascular Disease & Hypertension chapter*; signpost it, don't re-teach the BP/lipid science. Honest evidence gap: too few T2D DASH trials to rank it. *(`fang2025dash4d`, `shirani2013dash`, `jing2023networkma`.)*
13. **The PLATE METHOD is the practical, low-numeracy tool — HPB My Healthy Plate is the SG version** ("quarter, quarter, half"; water over sugary drinks), applied at the cai png / hawker stall. *(`hpbHealthyPlate`.)*
14. **Weight-centred remission of EARLY T2D is real (DiRECT) — but eligibility-bound and fades.** 46% at 12 months (early ≤6 y, non-insulin, defined BMI band), falling to 36% at 24 months and ~13% at 5 years; remission is weight-dependent and is deliberately called **remission, not cure**. *(`lean2018direct`, `direct5yr2024`, `riddle2021remission`, `diabetesUK2021remission`.)*
15. **The MECHANISM is the twin-cycle / personal-fat-threshold model** — fat inside liver and pancreas drives T2D; losing intra-organ fat reverses it. Relevant to Asians (more visceral fat at a given BMI). *Signpost depth.* *(`taylor2021remission`, `taylor2025twincycle`, `taylor2023pft`.)*
16. **The DELIVERY is supervised total diet replacement (TDR/VLCD)** — ~800–850 kcal/day formula phase, structured food reintroduction, clinician-led drug de-escalation on day 1 (glucose and BP fall fast). NOT a self-directed crash diet — recognise-and-refer to a structured programme. A 2026 SG expert consensus gives a local TDR framework with ethnicity-adjusted BMI thresholds. *(`lean2018direct`, `khoo2026sgDSF`.)*
17. **Remission generalises to Asians** (STANDby in South Asians; personal-fat-threshold in normal-BMI people) — so BMI entry bands should be **ethnicity-adjusted**, not the Western 27–45 applied unadjusted. Honest gap: no Singapore-population remission RCT yet. *(`standby2022southasian`, `taylor2023pft`, `khoo2026sgDSF`.)*
18. **Prevention works and beats a single conversation:** structured lifestyle cut progression 58% (metformin 31%) in DPP; the benefit persists for decades but attenuates; translation (NHS-DPP, and SG's Pre-DICTED) works at scale. *(`dpp2002`, `dppos2009`, `dppos2025_21yr`, `ali2012realworldDpp`, `mcmanus2022nhsDpp`, `bee2025predicted`.)*
19. **Guideline prediabetes advice + honest framing:** ~5–7% weight loss, ≥150 min/week, a quality pattern, refer to a structured programme — and **not everyone with prediabetes progresses** (some revert), so motivate without over-alarming (local: ≥35% progress over 8 years without change). *(`ada2026prevention`, `salasSalvado2014medDiet`, `imamura2015ssb`, `hpbPreDictedInfo`.)*
20. **Hypoglycaemia awareness is the safety spine:** identify the drug class FIRST (insulin/sulfonylureas/meglitinides = risk; metformin/DPP-4/SGLT2/GLP-1 alone = not). For fixed insulin, counsel **carbohydrate consistency**; treat a conscious hypo with the **"15-15" rule**; refer before endorsing low-carb/fasting. *(`ada2026glycemic`, `ada2026behaviors`, `aceT2DM2023`, `diabetesukHypo`, `ling2021elderlyhypo`.)*
21. **Ramadan fasting with diabetes (SG-relevant):** common, raises severe-hypo risk ~7.5-fold; risk-stratify and refer pre-Ramadan (IDF-DAR); the sick are religiously exempt; SG has home-grown tools (FAST RCT) and a documented knowledge gap. *(`salti2004epidiar`, `hassanein2022ramadan`, `endotext2022ramadan`, `lum2020fast`, `zainudin2022preparing`, `zainudin2017knowledge`.)*
22. **The Singapore War on Diabetes frames the whole chapter:** ~1 in 3 lifetime risk; ~9.1% current prevalence; a real ethnic gradient (Indians/Malays ~2× Chinese); policy levers (Healthier SG, screening, Nutri-Grade); ACE guidance to **signpost** drug classes. *(`mohNPHS2024`, `mohWarOnDiabetes`, `mohNNS2022`, `mohHealthierSG`, `mohHealthierSGScreening`, `mohNutriGradeFresh2023`, `aceT2DM2023`, `tan2020t2dm2050`.)*

## 6. Singapore context

- **The War on Diabetes is the chapter's opening frame.** MOH declared it in **April 2016** as a whole-of-nation effort; the headline figures a student should quote are **1 in 3 lifetime risk** and a projection to **1 million people with diabetes by 2050** on current trends (`mohWarOnDiabetes`). Current measured prevalence (**NPHS 2024**, released Oct 2025) is **9.1% crude / 8.8% age-standardised** among adults 18–74, **stable since 2010** (`mohNPHS2024`) — use these rather than older "400,000 / 1 in 9" numbers.
- **A real ethnic gradient.** NPHS 2024 measured **Indians 15.5%, Malays 15.0%, Chinese 7.4%**, with the gap starkest in women (Indian 14.7% / Malay 14.5% vs Chinese 6.2%) (`mohNPHS2024`). Frame this as *population-level* risk to guide screening priority — **never** "this ethnicity causes diabetes"; individual counselling stays individualised and non-judgemental. Pair with the **Asian BMI action points** owned by *the Obesity & Metabolic Syndrome chapter* (metabolic risk appears at lower weights).
- **The local food environment made concrete.** NNS 2022 (`mohNNS2022`): only **4% of staples are wholegrain**, **9 in 10** exceed the sodium recommendation, and **SSBs are 52% of dietary sugar** — the single biggest source. Teachable hawker swaps: white rice → **brown rice / smaller scoop + more vegetables**; teh tarik / bandung / sugarcane / bubble tea (often Nutri-Grade C/D) → **water or Grade A/B**, and kopi/teh **"kosong / siu dai"**; cai png "**more veg, one lean protein, less gravy**" to approximate My Healthy Plate (`hpbHealthyPlate`).
- **Carbohydrate quality is substitution, not rice-shaming.** The Singapore Chinese Health Study found **rice intake itself not associated with T2D** (HR 0.98); the win is **substituting wholemeal bread** (HR 0.82), not demonising rice (`seah2019scriphealthrice`, contrasted with the pooled-Asian `hu2012whiterice`). This is the more accurate — and less stigmatising — counselling message.
- **Policy levers students should recognise:** **Healthier SG** (enrolment from Jul 2023; a regular family doctor + Health Plan; `mohHealthierSG`); **Healthier SG Screening / Screen for Life** (subsidised diabetes screen via FPG or HbA1c — awareness level; `mohHealthierSGScreening`); **Nutri-Grade** (A–D by sugar **and saturated fat**; mandatory Dec 2022, extended to freshly-prepared drinks Dec 2023, Grade D advertising banned; `mohNutriGradeFresh2023`, `shin2023nutrigrade`); and **My Healthy Plate** (`hpbHealthyPlate`).
- **Local remission framework (honest status).** There is **no MOH/ACE national remission programme**; the local framework is **expert-consensus level** — a 2026 Singapore consensus endorses diabetes-specific-formula **TDR at 800–1,000 kcal/day × 12 weeks** for newly-diagnosed (≤6 y) T2D with overweight/obesity, with **ethnicity-adjusted BMI thresholds** (`khoo2026sgDSF`). State the gap plainly rather than implying a national remission programme exists.
- **Ramadan fasting is a Singapore-first counselling-safety task,** not a footnote — the community with the highest T2D burden (Malay-Muslim) is the one that observes the fast. SG has home-grown tools (the **FAST** RCT, `lum2020fast`; the SGH/SMJ pre-Ramadan approach, `zainudin2022preparing`) and a documented **knowledge gap** (`zainudin2017knowledge`). MUIS and imams reinforce that testing does not break the fast and that the sick are religiously exempt.
- **Local drug guidance is the signpost, not ADA/EASD alone.** ACE (MOH) publishes **T2DM — personalising management with non-insulin medications** (17 May 2023): metformin first-line; SGLT2i or GLP-1 RA for cardiorenal risk; patient-centred shared decisions (`aceT2DM2023`). Use it to say "drugs exist, they are personalised, prescribing is the team's job" while the student focuses on lifestyle and referral. An optional peer-reviewed projection quantifies the downstream complication burden (`tan2020t2dm2050`) — label it **modelled, not measured**.

## 7. Pillar intersections

- **Nutrition × physical activity.** Every prevention result here is a *diet + activity* package: DPP paired ~7% weight loss with **≥150 min/week** activity (`dpp2002`); ADA prediabetes advice repeats the ≥150 min/week target (`ada2026prevention`); Pre-DICTED delivered group lifestyle classes locally (`bee2025predicted`). Teach the two together and cross-reference the physical-activity content — activity independently improves insulin sensitivity and preserves lean mass during weight loss.
- **Nutrition × behaviour change / social connection (adherence).** The hard part of both DPP and DiRECT is *maintenance* — remission fades with regained weight (`direct5yr2024`), and translational programmes work through structure, group delivery, follow-up and lay educators (`ali2012realworldDpp`, `mcmanus2022nhsDpp`). Cross-reference *the Behaviour Change & Counselling chapter* for the motivational-interviewing / stages-of-change toolkit rather than re-teaching it here.
- **Nutrition × sleep/stress (one-line signpost).** Short sleep and chronic stress worsen insulin sensitivity and appetite regulation — a driver of intake and central adiposity. This axis is owned by *the Obesity & Metabolic Syndrome chapter*; mention it in one line and point there, do not develop it.
- **Nutrition × culture/faith (Ramadan).** The Ramadan subtopic is where nutrition, the physical stress of fasting, medication timing and religious observance intersect — the recognise-and-refer message is to risk-stratify and route to the diabetes team pre-Ramadan (`hassanein2022ramadan`, `zainudin2022preparing`).

## 8. Clinical application & case ideas (MCQ-only per house rule; do-no-harm aware)

*Per house rule, per-chapter cases are choice/MCQ only — the live AI patient-chat belongs only in the Integrated Cases chapter. Log finalised cases in `../cases/case-bank.md`.*

**Case A — Prediabetes: prevention counselling (DPP / Pre-DICTED logic).**
*Stem:* A 45-year-old Singaporean office worker (Malay, BMI 26, family history of T2DM) has an HbA1c in the prediabetes range on a Healthier SG screen. Asymptomatic.
- *MCQ 1 — "What is the best first step?"* ✔ Refer to a **structured lifestyle programme** + dietitian; aim ~5–7% weight loss + ≥150 min/wk; introduce My Healthy Plate and Nutri-Grade swaps. ✘ "Start metformin now" (lifestyle **outperformed** metformin in DPP, 58% vs 31%). ✘ "One-off advice to lose weight" (DPP's effect came from an *intensive, structured* programme; Pre-DICTED reproduced it locally). *Feedback teaches `dpp2002`, `bee2025predicted`, `ada2026prevention`.*
- *MCQ 2 — patient asks "Will I definitely get diabetes?"* ✔ Non-fatalistic framing: risk is modifiable, some revert to normal, ~35% progress over 8 years **without** change locally (`hpbPreDictedInfo`, `ada2026prevention`). ✘ "Yes, it's inevitable" (over-alarming, inaccurate).

**Case B — Early T2D: discussing remission realistically (DiRECT eligibility + durability).**
*Stem:* A 50-year-old (Indian, BMI 31, T2D diagnosed 2 years ago, on **metformin only, not on insulin**) asks, "I read I can reverse my diabetes — is that true?"
- *MCQ 1 — eligibility check:* ✔ "Realistic goal for someone diagnosed <6 years ago, not on insulin, in the studied BMI range — via a **supervised** weight-management programme; I'll refer you" (`lean2018direct`, `khoo2026sgDSF`). ✘ Promise remission without checking eligibility/duration.
- *MCQ 2 — "So I'll be cured?"* ✔ "**Remission, not cure** — it can fade (about half at 1 year, ~13% by 5 years) and depends on keeping weight off; we keep monitoring" (`direct5yr2024`, `riddle2021remission`, `diabetesUK2021remission`). ✘ "Yes, it's a permanent cure."
- *MCQ 3 — do-no-harm branch:* patient wants to **start the VLCD himself** and stop his metformin. ✔ "TDR is **clinician-supervised** — drugs are adjusted on day 1 because glucose and BP fall fast; don't self-titrate" (`lean2018direct`). ✘ "Sure, just buy meal-replacement shakes and stop your tablets."

**Case C — Hypoglycaemia safety before endorsing low-carb (drug-class recognition).**
*Stem:* A 68-year-old on **metformin + a sulfonylurea** with irregular hawker meals wants to "go low-carb / keto" after reading about it.
- *MCQ 1 — the safety step:* ✔ "**Check the drug class first** — a sulfonylurea can cause hypos if you cut/skip carbohydrate; refer to the diabetes team to review medication **before** restricting carbs" (`ada2026glycemic`, `aceT2DM2023`, `ling2021elderlyhypo`). ✘ "Low-carb is always safe — go ahead."
- *MCQ 2 — treating a hypo:* which is correct first-line for a conscious hypo? ✔ ~15 g **fast-acting glucose**, recheck in 15 min, repeat if still low ("15-15"). ✘ Chocolate/peanuts (fat/protein slow absorption) (`ada2026glycemic`, `diabetesukHypo`).
- *Contrast trap:* a **metformin-only** patient asking the same question → low-carb reduction is reasonable (no intrinsic hypo risk), still refer to a dietitian.

**Case D — Ramadan fasting with T2D (recognise-and-refer, faith-aware).**
*Stem:* A 55-year-old Malay man with T2D on **insulin** plans to fast for Ramadan and says "testing my sugar breaks my fast, so I won't check."
- *MCQ 1 — correct the misconception:* ✔ "Finger-prick glucose testing does **not** break the fast; and Islam exempts the medically unfit — let's arrange a **pre-Ramadan review** with the diabetes team" (`zainudin2017knowledge`, `hassanein2022ramadan`). ✘ "Fasting on insulin is fine, no special preparation needed."
- *MCQ 2 — the risk:* which patient is **highest** priority for pre-Ramadan referral? ✔ insulin/sulfonylurea-treated with prior severe hypo or hypo unawareness (`hassanein2022ramadan`, `salti2004epidiar`).

## 9. Common misconceptions & pitfalls

- **"There's a single best diabetes diet / a magic macronutrient ratio."** → No ideal % from carbohydrate/protein/fat; several patterns are acceptable; quality + individualised MNT + dietitian referral (`ada2026standards`, `evert2019nutrition`, `dyson2018diabetesuk`).
- **"Glycaemic index is a precise rule."** → GI is measured for single foods eaten alone; mixed meals and inter-individual variability blunt it — prefer wholegrain/high-fibre choices over memorising GI tables (`chiavaroli2021gigl`, `dnsg2023easd`).
- **"Rice is the enemy."** → Locally, rice intake was **not** associated with T2D; the win is **substitution** toward wholegrain (`seah2019scriphealthrice`) — a more accurate, less stigmatising message than banning rice.
- **"Sugar-free drinks and fruit juice are fine."** → Each SSB serving ≈ +13–18% T2D risk (partly weight-independent); fruit juice is **not** a protective alternative; use Nutri-Grade and water-first (`imamura2015ssb`, `shin2023nutrigrade`).
- **"Remission = cure."** → Remission is HbA1c <6.5% sustained ≥3 months **off** drugs; it fades (~13% at 5 y in DiRECT), is not permanent, and monitoring continues — the word is deliberately **remission, not cure** (`riddle2021remission`, `diabetesUK2021remission`, `direct5yr2024`).
- **"Anyone with T2D can reverse it."** → DiRECT enrolled **early** disease only (≤6 y, non-insulin, defined BMI band); long-standing/insulin-treated disease is different, and low-carb "reversal" is not the same as consensus remission (`lean2018direct`, `athinarayanan2019virta2yr`).
- **"Just apply DiRECT's BMI 27–45 band to Singaporeans."** → Those are **Western** cut-offs; Asians cross their threshold at lower BMI — use **ethnicity-adjusted** thresholds (`taylor2023pft`, `khoo2026sgDSF`, `standby2022southasian`).
- **"Telling a patient to lose weight is enough (prevention)."** → DPP's result came from an **intensive 16-session structured programme** + activity + follow-up, reproduced locally by Pre-DICTED — not a single conversation (`dpp2002`, `bee2025predicted`).
- **"Low-carb is always safe / always durable."** → It is an **option** with ~6-month benefit that **attenuates by 12 months**; and it is **unsafe to endorse before checking the drug class** — a sulfonylurea or insulin needs medication review first (`goldenberg2021lowcarb`, `ada2026glycemic`).
- **"PREDIMED proves a Mediterranean diet controls blood sugar."** → PREDIMED is a **CVD-prevention** trial (composite MI/stroke/CV death), **retracted and republished (2018)** for randomisation flaws — it supports *pattern* quality, **not** glycaemic control; CVD-risk diet is owned by *the Cardiovascular Disease & Hypertension chapter* (`estruch2018predimed`).
- **"Testing my glucose breaks my fast" (Ramadan).** → It does **not**; the sick are religiously exempt; risk-stratify and refer pre-Ramadan (`zainudin2017knowledge`, `hassanein2022ramadan`).
- **"GLP-1 RAs / new drugs replace diet."** → Pharmacology **complements**, not replaces, lifestyle; it is **signposted** here, lifestyle stays foundational (`aceT2DM2023`).

## 11. Evidence gaps / open questions / residual verify notes

**Content / evidence gaps (state honestly in the chapter):**
- **Durability of dietary remission beyond 5 years**, and how to *maintain* it in real-world (non-trial) primary care, remain uncertain — DiRECT's 5-year ~13% shows the maintenance problem is unsolved (`direct5yr2024`).
- **No Singapore-population remission RCT exists** — the Asian-generalisability evidence is STANDby (UK South Asians, `standby2022southasian`) plus the Singapore **expert consensus** (`khoo2026sgDSF`), not a local cohort; and there is **no MOH/ACE national remission/TDR programme** (ACE 2023 is lifestyle-first + drug signposting only). Do not imply a national remission programme exists.
- **DASH in T2D is under-powered:** a network meta-analysis could not rank DASH for HbA1c for lack of eligible T2D trials (`jing2023networkma`) — an honest gap; DASH's BP/lipid rationale is owned by *the Cardiovascular Disease & Hypertension chapter*.
- **Not all prediabetes progresses** (some revert; `ada2026prevention`) — keep counselling motivating, not fatalistic.
