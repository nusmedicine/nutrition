# Interprofessional Practice, Referral and Practitioner Self-Care — research dossier

## 1. Scope (one paragraph)
This capstone chapter consolidates the professional-identity strand of a first-year, nutrition-led curriculum. Having learned *what* good dietary care looks like in earlier chapters, students now learn *where their own role ends and someone else's begins*. The chapter teaches first-year clinicians to deliver brief, evidence-based nutrition support **within scope**, to recognise the triggers that mandate referral to a registered dietitian (RD) for medical nutrition therapy (MNT), and to write a concise, useful referral. It situates this in Singapore's **Healthier SG (HSG)** model — one enrolled family doctor, a shared Health Plan, team-based and community ("social prescribing") support, and the **Healthier SG Chronic Tier** funding framework — as the concrete local example of structured, interprofessional chronic-disease care. Finally, it asks students to turn the lens on themselves: practitioner self-care, sustainable diet/sleep/activity habits, and the evidence that a clinician's own health behaviours are associated with both their counselling credibility and their professional longevity. The throughline is humility-with-competence: know your limits, refer well, work in a team, and look after yourself.

## 2. Learning objectives (refined)
1. **Define the scope and boundaries** of a first-year clinician's role in nutrition care — distinguish brief lifestyle advice and patient education (in scope) from individualised medical nutrition therapy (out of scope; RD-led).
2. **Identify referral triggers** and demonstrate **when and how to refer** a patient to a registered dietitian for MNT, including writing a concise, complete dietitian referral.
3. **Explain the value of interprofessional, team-based nutrition care**, naming core team roles (family doctor, RD, nurse, pharmacist, allied health, community partners) and how they coordinate.
4. **Describe Singapore's Healthier SG model and Chronic Tier** as a structured chronic-disease pathway, including how enrolment, the Health Plan, social prescribing and subsidy/funding shape access to nutrition-related care.
5. **Reflect on one's own diet, sleep and activity** as professional role-modelling and a possible determinant of counselling credibility, and outline a personal self-care/sustainability plan (signposting that burnout management is a longitudinal professional skill).

## 3. Scope boundaries
**In:**
- Scope-of-practice concepts: what a first-year clinician may safely advise vs. must refer.
- Referral triggers and the mechanics of a good dietitian referral.
- The RD role and a *plain-language* definition of MNT (the Nutrition Care Process named, not drilled).
- Interprofessional/team-based care principles and core roles.
- Healthier SG: enrolment, Health Plan, team/community partners, social prescribing, Chronic Tier funding pathway (orientation level).
- Practitioner self-care, professionalism, and role-modelling evidence.

**Out (covered elsewhere / future learning):**
- Detailed MNT prescribing, therapeutic diets, enteral/parenteral nutrition, condition-specific macro/micronutrient targets → later clinical years; **signpost only**.
- Disease-specific guideline thresholds (e.g., ACE T2DM/lipid targets) → earlier disease chapters and future years; cite for orientation, not memorisation.
- Motivational interviewing / behaviour-change technique depth → counselling-skills chapter.
- Detailed health-financing/policy mechanics, billing codes, MediSave/MediShield rules → health-systems teaching; HSG covered at orientation depth only.
- Burnout diagnosis/management and physician mental-health pathways → professional-development/wellbeing curriculum; introduced here, not treated.

## 4. Key concepts to teach (didactic spine)
1. **Professional identity = competence + boundaries.** A safe clinician is defined as much by what they refer as by what they do. First-year nutrition care is brief, general, evidence-aligned, and patient-centred. [aclm2022comp; afn2021curriculum]
2. **The "advise vs. treat" line.** General healthy-eating guidance (My Healthy Plate, reduce sugary drinks, dietary patterns) is universal advice; **individualised therapeutic nutrition for a medical condition is MNT** and belongs to the RD. [ada2024care; hpbHealthyPlate]
3. **Referral triggers — a memorable checklist.** Refer to an RD when: complex/multiple conditions (T2DM, CKD, CVD, GI disease), unintended weight loss or malnutrition risk, obesity needing structured management, pregnancy/paediatric nutrition, food allergy/intolerance, eating-disorder concern, or when advice exceeds general guidance or the patient isn't progressing. [ada2024care; aclm2022comp]
4. **What MNT is (plain language).** RD-led, individualised assessment–diagnosis–intervention–monitoring (the Nutrition Care Process), evidence-based and outcome-tracked; for diabetes, RD-delivered MNT is associated with HbA1c reductions of roughly 0.3–2.0% in T2DM (guideline-reported range across trials of varying intensity, not a guaranteed effect). [ada2024care]
5. **How to write a good referral.** Reason for referral, relevant diagnoses, current meds, key labs (e.g., HbA1c, lipids, renal function), weight/BMI trend, patient goals/social context, and urgency. A vague referral wastes a scarce resource. [ada2024care]
6. **Interprofessional, team-based care.** Roles and hand-offs: family doctor (coordinator), RD (MNT), nurse (monitoring/education), pharmacist (med review), and community/allied partners. Team-based care is associated with improved chronic-disease outcomes; nutrition is rarely a solo act. [aclm2022comp; afn2021curriculum]
7. **Structured chronic-disease pathways — Healthier SG.** One enrolled family doctor + shared Health Plan + community partners (HPB, PA, SportSG, Active Ageing Centres) + **social prescribing** + **Chronic Tier** subsidies for chronic medications and care components. The local scaffold that makes referral and follow-up actually happen. [Singapore context §6]
8. **Self-care, professionalism and role-modelling.** Clinicians who look after their own diet/activity are observed to counsel more, and arguably more credibly; sustainable self-care plausibly protects both the patient interaction and the career (associational evidence — see §11). [lobelo2015rolemodel; lianov2010]
9. **The honesty caveat.** First-years report feeling *under-prepared* in nutrition; recognising this gap is itself a professional competency that justifies referral and lifelong learning. [crowley2019]

## 6. Singapore context
**Healthier SG (HSG)** is the national shift to preventive, primary-care-anchored health (White Paper 2022; enrolment from 5 July 2023). Core features to teach at orientation level:
- **One enrolled family doctor** for residents (initially 40+), who co-develops a **personalised Health Plan** covering screenings, vaccinations and lifestyle/health goals — the natural place a nutrition concern is raised and **referred**.
- **Team-based + community model.** HSG is delivered through regional health clusters partnering **HPB**, the Agency for Integrated Care, People's Association, Sport Singapore and Active Ageing Centres. Family doctors can make **social prescriptions** — referrals to community programmes (e.g., HPB activities) supporting healthier living. This is the concrete local example of interprofessional, beyond-the-clinic care.
- **Healthier SG Chronic Tier (from 1 Feb 2024) — the funding pathway to teach.** CHAS / Pioneer Generation / Merdeka Generation cardholders enrolled at their HSG GP get means-tested subsidies of **up to 87.5%** on selected chronic medications (no dollar cap) plus up to **$360/year** toward other care components (consultation, lab tests, other medications). *(Verified against MOH/CHAS Feb-2024 announcements.)* CHAS clinics (including HSG GPs) also provide **subsidised referrals to public Specialist Outpatient Clinics**. *Honest caveat for students:* the public HSG/Chronic-Tier materials emphasise medications and care components; they do **not** spell out a dedicated dietitian/MNT subsidy line — RD/allied-health access typically flows via polyclinic/SOC/cluster allied-health services and the broader CDMP, and exact funding evolves. Teach the **pathway and the principle**, and flag funding detail as something to verify against current MOH/CHAS pages.
- **Local epidemiology anchors.** War on Diabetes; National Nutrition Survey 2022 shows ~9 in 10 Singaporeans exceed sodium guidance and only ~4% choose wholegrain staples — exactly the population-level problems that need *both* universal advice (in scope) *and* RD referral (out of scope) at the individual level. [mohWarOnDiabetes; mohNNS2022]
- **Hawker-food examples for the "advise vs refer" line.** A first-year can advise: choose *thinner* gravy, ask for less rice, swap to teh-si-kosong, pick a Nutri-Grade A/B drink, add a side of vegetables (cai png with 2 veg + 1 protein ≈ My Healthy Plate). A first-year should **refer** the newly-diagnosed T2DM uncle on three agents with an HbA1c of 9% and CKD, who needs an individualised carbohydrate/renal plan — that is MNT. [hpbHealthyPlate; hpbNutriGrade]

## 7. Pillar intersections
- **Practitioner personal-health pillar.** The chapter operationalises the lifestyle-medicine principle that the clinician is a participant, not just a prescriber: own diet, sleep and activity are both a wellbeing issue and a counselling-credibility issue. [lianov2010; lobelo2015rolemodel; sleepRestriction]
- **Cross-pillar interprofessional collaboration.** Nutrition rarely acts alone — referral and team care connect the nutrition pillar to physical activity (SportSG, social prescribing), sleep, stress/behavioural support, and medical management. The RD referral is the canonical cross-pillar hand-off, and HSG is the cross-pillar delivery vehicle.

## 8. Clinical application & case ideas (case-player-ready)

**Case A — "Refer or advise? Write the referral."**
Setup: Mdm Tan, 58, HSG-enrolled, newly diagnosed T2DM, HbA1c 9.1%, eGFR 52, on metformin + two agents, BMI 29 (Asian cut-offs → obese), eats mostly hawker food, lives alone. [sgObesityBMI; mohWarOnDiabetes]
- *Branch 1 — student gives a printed My Healthy Plate sheet and stops.* → Feedback: appropriate universal advice, **but insufficient**; complexity (T2DM + early CKD + polypharmacy) crosses the MNT line. Prompts to escalate.
- *Branch 2 — student refers to RD but writes "please advise on diet."* → Feedback: referral made (good) but **uninformative**; case-player asks them to add diagnoses, HbA1c, eGFR, meds, BMI/weight trend, goals, social context, urgency.
- *Branch 3 — student writes a complete referral and continues brief supportive advice in the meantime.* → Best path: models "refer well **and** stay engaged within scope," plus mentions HSG Chronic Tier so the patient understands subsidised follow-up.
- Optional twist: RD wait-list is long → student uses community/social-prescribing option and safe interim advice rather than overstepping into MNT.
- *Player-state variables (for branching):* `referral_made` (bool), `referral_complete` (bool — diagnoses+labs+meds+goals present), `interim_advice_in_scope` (bool), `hsg_pathway_mentioned` (bool). Score = completeness × stayed-in-scope.

**Case B — "Physician, mind thyself."**
Setup: First-year on a packed posting; skips meals, lives on kopi and economy bee hoon, 5 h sleep, no exercise; about to counsel a patient on lifestyle change.
- *Branch 1 — "do as I say, not as I do," ignores own habits.* → Feedback surfaces the role-model evidence (own habits associated with counselling rate/credibility — associational) and the sleep–appetite/insulin link affecting the student's own health. [lobelo2015rolemodel; sleepRestriction]
- *Branch 2 — student writes a realistic, sustainable self-care micro-plan* (one swap, a sleep anchor, a short walk) and reflects on how lived experience builds empathy and credibility. → Best path; ties to professionalism and longevity, signposts that burnout is a longitudinal skill, not a one-off fix.
- *Player-state variables:* `acknowledged_own_habits` (bool), `plan_is_realistic` (bool — single small change, not overhaul), `linked_to_credibility` (bool).
- Reflection capture: short free-text on "one boundary I'll keep, one referral I'll make, one self-care habit I'll start."

## 9. Common misconceptions & pitfalls
- **"More knowledge = I can manage it myself."** Knowing nutrition science is not a licence to deliver MNT; scope is about role and accountability, not just facts. [aclm2022comp]
- **"Referral = failure / giving up."** Appropriate referral is a *competency and a safety behaviour*, not a deficiency. [ada2024care]
- **"A referral note can be one line."** Vague referrals waste a scarce, valuable resource and delay care; completeness is a clinical skill.
- **"Any dietary advice is MNT."** General healthy-eating guidance is *not* MNT; conflating them either inflates scope or paralyses students from giving safe basic advice.
- **"DiRECT/DPP-style results come from a clinic chat."** Those outcomes required **intensive, structured, multi-session, team-delivered** programmes (DiRECT's total-diet-replacement protocol; DPP's 16-session curriculum) in **selected** populations (DiRECT: early ≤6 y T2DM, BMI 27–45, non-insulin). Don't promise remission from advice alone. [lean2018direct; dpp2002]
- **"PREDIMED proves a Mediterranean diet cuts *my* heart attacks by a third."** The ~30% figure is a **relative** risk reduction on a **composite** endpoint, was **stroke-dominated**, and comes from the **2018 re-analysis** after the original randomisation was corrected — population-level association, not an individual guarantee. [estruch2018predimed]
- **"Self-care is indulgent."** Practitioner wellbeing is a professional and patient-safety issue, not a luxury. [lianov2010]
- **"Singapore funds RD visits the same as GP meds."** Don't assume; HSG Chronic Tier subsidy detail centres on medications/care components — verify current allied-health funding before quoting figures to a patient.

## 11. Evidence gaps / open questions
- **Local funding specifics:** The exact, current subsidy pathway for RD/MNT under Healthier SG / Chronic Tier / CDMP is not clearly published in patient-facing materials — needs verification against live MOH/CHAS/cluster sources and may change. The Chronic Tier headline figures (up to 87.5% on selected chronic meds, no dollar cap; up to $360/yr other care components; from 1 Feb 2024) were web-verified against MOH/CHAS for this dossier, but allied-health/dietitian funding remains unspecified. Teach principle over numbers.
- **Optimal referral thresholds:** Evidence-based, validated triggers for "GP-managed vs RD-managed" nutrition care are largely consensus-based, not trial-derived.
- **Role-model evidence is associational:** Clinician-habit ↔ counselling links (lobelo2015rolemodel) are observational; causal/intervention data are thinner. Present as plausible, not proven — wording in §1, §4.8, §7, §8B softened accordingly.
- **First-year self-care outcomes:** Limited longitudinal evidence on whether early self-care/role-model training changes later practice behaviour or patient outcomes.
- **Team-care attribution:** Hard to isolate the RD/nutrition contribution within multi-component team interventions (e.g., DiRECT) — caution against over-claiming the nutrition slice.
- **ADA edition:** `ada2024care` MNT figures and "refer to dietitian" guidance should be re-confirmed against the live 2025/2026 Standards of Care edition before publication.
