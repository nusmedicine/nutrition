# Overnutrition: Overweight, Obesity and Metabolic Syndrome — research dossier

## 1. Scope (one paragraph)
This chapter teaches first-year medical students to **recognise, classify and explain** overweight, obesity and the metabolic syndrome as the dominant overnutrition phenotypes in Singapore, and to frame their management as **lifestyle-first** within a multi-pillar lifestyle-medicine model. The Singapore-first anchor is the **Asian-specific BMI action points** (≥23 kg/m² for intervention/"overweight at risk"; ≥27.5 kg/m² as a higher-risk action point), set against the WHO global cut-offs (≥25, ≥30), because Asians carry more body fat (and visceral fat) at a given BMI and develop cardiometabolic disease at lower BMI. Students learn the components of metabolic syndrome and how they cluster around central adiposity and insulin resistance; how positive energy balance translates into fat gain; and how two often-neglected pillars — **sleep and stress** — bias energy intake, appetite hormones, cortisol-driven appetite and visceral fat deposition. Pharmacotherapy and bariatric/metabolic surgery are **signposted only** as specialist/future learning; the teaching depth stops at classify/explain plus first-line lifestyle and behavioural intervention.

## 2. Learning objectives (refined)
By the end of the chapter, students can:
1. **Classify** an Asian patient's weight status using Singapore's BMI action points (≥23, ≥27.5) plus waist circumference, and **explain** physiologically why these are stricter than WHO cut-offs (higher body-fat % / comorbidity risk at lower BMI; locally validated by the Yishun Study).
2. **Describe** the five components of metabolic syndrome (central adiposity, raised triglycerides, low HDL, raised blood pressure, raised fasting glucose) and **explain** how they cluster around insulin resistance and visceral fat rather than occurring independently.
3. **Explain** weight gain as cumulative positive energy balance modulated by diet quality, physical activity, sleep and stress — and **justify** why lifestyle/behavioural modification is first-line, signposting where pharmacotherapy/surgery are added.
4. **Outline** how short/poor sleep and chronic stress intersect with appetite regulation, cortisol and central adiposity, and **identify** these as modifiable contributors in a real patient.

*(Verb level intentionally capped at recognise/classify/describe/explain/outline — no "manage with drug X" objectives at first year.)*

## 3. Scope boundaries
**In:** Asian BMI action points and rationale; waist circumference / central obesity (Asian cut-offs ~90 cm men, ~80 cm women); metabolic syndrome harmonised components and clustering; energy balance applied to weight gain; lifestyle-first weight management (diet pattern, activity, sleep, stress, behaviour change); the sleep–stress–cortisol–visceral fat axis; Singapore epidemiology and HPB/MOH instruments; honest dating of the 2016 Obesity CPG.

**Out (covered elsewhere):** Detailed T2DM diagnosis/management and "War on Diabetes" screening (diabetes/dysglycaemia chapter — cross-reference `mohWarOnDiabetes`, `aceT2DM2023`); dyslipidaemia drug targets (`aceLipid2023`); MASLD/fatty liver mechanisms; **anti-obesity pharmacotherapy (e.g. GLP-1 receptor agonists) and bariatric/metabolic surgery** — *signposted as future/specialist learning only*; undernutrition and eating disorders; childhood obesity specifics; obesity genetics/monogenic syndromes.

## 4. Key concepts to teach (didactic spine)
1. **Overnutrition is a pattern, not a moral failing** — chronic positive energy balance + obesogenic environment; sets a non-stigmatising frame. *(tie: `who2020diet`, `aha2021diet`)*
2. **BMI is a screening proxy with population-specific cut-offs** — it estimates adiposity imperfectly; Singaporean adults carry higher body fat % at a given BMI than older local and Caucasian cohorts, so Singapore acts earlier. *(tie: `sgObesityBMI`; Yishun Study)*
3. **Singapore's action points: ≥23 (intervene) and ≥27.5 (higher-risk action)** — two points below WHO ≥25/≥30; pair BMI with **waist circumference** because central fat is the key risk driver. *(tie: `sgObesityBMI`)*
4. **Central (visceral) adiposity drives cardiometabolic risk** — visceral fat is metabolically active and is associated with insulin resistance and dyslipidaemia. *(tie: `sgObesityBMI`, `metsHarmonized2009`)*
5. **Metabolic syndrome = clustering of ≥3 of 5 components** around insulin resistance/central obesity (harmonised 2009 definition; population-specific waist cut-offs). It identifies elevated CVD/T2DM risk rather than being a single disease. *(tie: `metsHarmonized2009`; `mohWarOnDiabetes`)*
6. **Energy balance applied to weight gain** — small chronic surpluses accumulate; diet *quality* and ultra-processed/sugary intake matter, not just calories. *(tie: `who2020diet`, `hpbNutriGrade`, `aha2021diet`)*
7. **Lifestyle/behavioural modification is first-line** — modest sustained weight loss (~5–10%) yields disproportionate metabolic benefit; pharmacotherapy/surgery are *added*, not substituted, in specialist settings. *(tie: `sgObesityBMI`, `dpp2002`, `lean2018direct`)*
8. **Sleep is a metabolic regulator** — short/poor sleep lowers insulin sensitivity and shifts appetite hormones (↑ghrelin, ↓leptin), increasing intake. *(tie: `sleepRestriction`)*
9. **Stress drives comfort-food intake and visceral fat** — cortisol pathways favour energy-dense food and abdominal fat deposition (mechanistic; mindfulness/stress-reduction trials are exploratory). *(tie: `dallman2003`)*
10. **Behaviour change is learnable** — motivational interviewing improves dietary adherence; stages-of-change is a useful heuristic, not a guaranteed lever. *(tie: `frost2018mi`, `mastellos2014ttm`)*

## 6. Singapore context
- **Action points and instruments.** Singapore's preventive protocol (`sgObesityBMI`) and the underlying **HPB-MOH Obesity CPG (1/2016)** set the Asian cut-offs: BMI **≥23 kg/m²** = increased risk ("overweight"), **≥27.5 kg/m²** = high risk, with waist circumference as a co-criterion for central obesity. **Honest dating:** the anchoring CPG dates to **2016** and has not been comprehensively re-issued. It remains the national document for the BMI action points, but its **pharmacotherapy/surgery content predates GLP-1-era practice** and the current obesity treatment landscape. Teach the **cut-offs as current** while flagging explicitly that the **management sections are dated** and superseded by newer specialist guidance (see e.g. the 2023 *SMJ* "current treatment landscape" commentary).
- **Population obesity is rising.** Per MOH/HPB **National Population Health Survey (NPHS 2024)**, obesity prevalence (WHO threshold BMI ≥30.0 kg/m²) rose **significantly from 10.5% (2019–2020) to 12.7% (2023–2024)** *(verified against the MOH newsroom release)*. Teaching note: Singapore reports *population* obesity at WHO ≥30 for international comparability, but applies *clinical* action at ≥27.5 — a deliberate, teachable distinction (surveillance threshold ≠ clinical action point).
- **Ethnic risk gradient.** Malay and Indian residents carry higher overweight/obesity and diabetes burden (link to `mohWarOnDiabetes`) — relevant for case framing and avoiding one-size-fits-all assumptions.
- **Policy levers students should know:** **My Healthy Plate** (`hpbHealthyPlate`), **Nutri-Grade** beverage grading (`hpbNutriGrade`), the Healthier Choice Symbol, and active-living programmes.
- **Hawker-food worked examples (energy/quality anchors):** char kway teow, nasi lemak, roti prata with curry, mee goreng, fried carrot cake = energy-dense, high refined-carb/oil; bubble tea / kopi-C / teh = Nutri-Grade C/D sugar load. **Lower-density swaps:** thosai over prata, yong tau foo (clear soup, less fried items, more vegetables/wholegrain noodles), sliced fish soup, *teh-O kosong* (no sugar), "less rice / half rice + extra vegetables," kopi/teh siu dai or kosong. These make energy balance and `who2020diet` sugar limits concrete and locally credible.

## 7. Pillar intersections
- **nutrition × sleep.** Short/fragmented sleep (and shift work) lowers insulin sensitivity and shifts appetite hormones (↑ghrelin, ↓leptin) → larger, more carbohydrate-heavy late-day intake; chronic mismatch is associated with weight gain and central adiposity (`sleepRestriction`). Sleep is a *modifiable* weight lever, not background noise. (Note: hormone-direction findings are consistent for ghrelin/hunger in the core experimental work but vary across observational studies — teach directionally.)
- **nutrition × stress (cortisol / comfort food).** Chronic stress activates cortisol pathways that bias choice toward energy-dense "comfort" foods and are associated with visceral fat deposition (`dallman2003`). Teach stress as a driver of *what and how much* people eat — but present mindfulness/stress-reduction trial evidence honestly as **exploratory**.
- **nutrition × physical activity.** Activity contributes to energy expenditure and, importantly, improves insulin sensitivity and helps preserve lean mass during weight loss — but diet quality is the larger lever for weight; pair the two (`dpp2002` lifestyle arm combined diet + activity).

## 8. Clinical application & case ideas (case-player-ready)

**Case A — "Mr Tan, 45, BMI 28, central obesity."** *(flagship branching case)*
- *Stem:* Office worker, BMI 28 kg/m², waist 98 cm, BP 138/86, slightly raised fasting glucose, low HDL. Eats hawker lunches daily (char kway teow, kopi-C twice/day), <5,000 steps, sleeps ~6 h.
- *Branch 1 — Classify:* Choose weight category. ✔ "Obese by Singapore action point (≥27.5)" / ✘ "Normal — BMI <30" (teaches WHO vs Asian cut-off; central obesity by waist confirms risk).
- *Branch 2 — Metabolic syndrome check:* Tally components (central obesity + ↓HDL + ↑BP + ↑fasting glucose = ≥3 → meets criteria). Reinforces clustering (`metsHarmonized2009`).
- *Branch 3 — Find multi-pillar contributors:* Identify nutrition (energy-dense hawker meals, sugary kopi), activity (sedentary), **sleep** (6 h), and **stress** (deadline snacking) as levers. ✔ multi-pillar plan / ✘ "prescribe weight-loss drug now" → feedback: lifestyle is first-line; pharmacotherapy is specialist/future learning.
- *Branch 4 — First-line plan:* Pick realistic swaps (half-rice + vegetables, *kopi-O kosong*, lunchtime walk, sleep target), set ~5–10% weight-loss goal; introduce MI framing (`frost2018mi`).

**Case B — "Ms Sarah, 32, rotating-shift nurse."**
- *Stem:* BMI 26, normal waist trending up, stress-related snacking on night shifts, irregular sleep, frequent bubble tea (Nutri-Grade D).
- *Branch 1 — Mechanism:* Link short/disrupted sleep → ↑ghrelin/↓leptin and cortisol-driven cravings (`sleepRestriction`, `dallman2003`).
- *Branch 2 — Prioritise pillar:* Sleep/circadian stabilisation + planned night-shift meals vs "just eat less" → feedback that addressing the upstream driver beats willpower messaging.
- *Branch 3 — Beverage swap:* Nutri-Grade as a decision aid; choose lower-sugar options.
- *Pitfall trap:* Selecting blame/shame counselling → stigma feedback loop (non-stigmatising framing).

## 9. Common misconceptions & pitfalls
- "BMI 28 is fine — obesity is BMI ≥30." → Conflates WHO with Singapore action points; ≥27.5 is the local high-risk threshold.
- "BMI measures fat directly / is enough on its own." → BMI is a proxy; pair with waist circumference; muscular or older patients mislead BMI (Yishun Study shows higher body fat % at a given BMI locally).
- "The 2016 CPG is the current standard for *everything* in obesity." → Cut-offs persist, but its **management** content is dated (pre-GLP-1 era); date it honestly.
- "Metabolic syndrome is one disease." → It's a *clustering* of risk factors (≥3 of 5) around insulin resistance — a risk flag, not a diagnosis with a single treatment.
- "Weight is purely calories in/out and willpower." → Sleep, stress, environment and food quality modulate intake and fat distribution; framing matters and stigma harms.
- "Start drugs/surgery early." → First-year stops at lifestyle-first; pharmacotherapy/bariatric surgery are signposted specialist options.
- "Trials prove diet cures diabetes for everyone." → **DiRECT's ~46% remission applies to early (≤6 y), non-insulin, BMI 27–45 patients in a structured open-label programme**; **DPP's 58% came from an intensive 16-session programme**, not a single clinic visit. Neither is a universal "cure," and generalisability to multi-ethnic Asian primary care is not established.
- "A Mediterranean-style diet definitively slashes heart attacks." → **PREDIMED reports a ~30% *relative* risk reduction on a *composite* endpoint that was stroke-dominated**, from a **2018 re-analysis after the original 2013 paper was retracted and republished** (randomisation irregularities). Use it to support dietary *pattern* quality, not as proof of a large absolute MI reduction.

## 10. Proposed interactive elements
- **Quiz topics:** (a) classify BMI 24 / 27 / 29 Asian vs WHO; (b) pick which 3 findings meet metabolic-syndrome criteria; (c) match sleep/stress mechanism to outcome (↑ghrelin, visceral fat); (d) "first-line vs signposted" sorter (lifestyle vs drug/surgery).
- **Diagram idea:** a **"clustering wheel"** — central adiposity/insulin resistance at the hub with the five metabolic-syndrome spokes; an overlay panel showing how sleep loss and stress feed the hub. Second simple figure: **WHO vs Singapore BMI number-line** (23 / 25 / 27.5 / 30 marked).
- **Case:** Mr Tan (Case A) as the flagship branching case, with the multi-pillar contributor hunt as the core interaction.

## 11. Evidence gaps / open questions
- **Citation keys to reconcile / add (do before formal citation):**
  - `sgObesityBMI` in the shared library currently points to the **Healthier SG / Primary Care Pages BMI-control protocol page (2016)**, *not* to the journal CPG. The Asian cut-offs and body-fat rationale ultimately derive from the **HPB-MOH Obesity CPG 1/2016** (*Singapore Med J*; **PubMed 27353244 — verified**). Add a distinct key for the CPG (e.g. `hpbmoh2016obesityCPG`) rather than overloading `sgObesityBMI`.
  - **Yishun Study** (`BMC Public Health` 2021; PMC8170923; verified) is cited as local validation but has no library key — add one (e.g. `tay2021yishun`) before citing.
  - `metsHarmonized2009` (Alberti et al., *Circulation* 2009;120:1640–1645; verified) is **not yet in the .bib** — add it.
  - `sleepRestriction` is referenced throughout but has **no .bib entry**; the underlying source is Spiegel et al. (*Ann Intern Med* 2004; companion Taheri *PLOS Med* 2004) — add a concrete entry.
- **Aging anchor guideline:** the Singapore Obesity CPG dates to **2016**; no comprehensive national re-issue captures GLP-1-era practice — flag for update when MOH/ACE publishes newer guidance.
- **Central-obesity epidemiology:** NPHS reports BMI-based obesity (≥30); published waist-circumference/central-obesity prevalence trend data are less prominent — a local data gap.
- **Sleep/stress hormone findings vary** across study designs (leptin direction in particular); teach mechanism directionally, not as fixed numbers.
- **Behaviour-change tools:** MI has reasonable adherence evidence (`frost2018mi`); Stages-of-Change has weak weight-outcome evidence (`mastellos2014ttm`) — present honestly.
- **Generalisability of remission/prevention trials** (DiRECT, DPP) and pattern trials (PREDIMED) to multi-ethnic Asian primary care and to higher baseline BMI is not fully established.
