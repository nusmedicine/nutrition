## 1. Scope (one paragraph)

This Part IV chapter teaches the first-year medical student to **recognise, classify and explain** overweight, obesity and the metabolic syndrome as the dominant **over-nutrition phenotypes**, and to frame their care as **lifestyle-first, weight-inclusive, and referral-aware**. It opens the **cardiometabolic-renal cascade** and is the narrative bookend to the *Undernutrition & Malnutrition* chapter — the two tails of malnutrition. The Singapore-first anchor is the **Asian-specific BMI action points** — intervene at **≥23 kg/m²**, treat as high-risk at **≥27.5 kg/m²** — shifted down from the WHO global **≥25 / ≥30**, because Asians carry more body fat (and proportionally more **visceral/ectopic fat**) at a given BMI and cross diabetes/CVD risk thresholds at a lower BMI; the locally validated **Yishun Study** supplies the number. Students learn **waist circumference** (Asian cut-offs ≥90 cm men / ≥80 cm women) as the bedside marker of central adiposity; the **harmonised 2009 metabolic syndrome** as a **clustering of ≥3 of 5** components around **insulin resistance and central adiposity** — a *risk marker*, not a standalone treatable disease; how **chronic positive energy balance** translates to fat gain, with the pivot from *calories* to **diet quality** (ultra-processed foods, sugary drinks); and **lifestyle-first management** across five pillars — diet pattern, activity, sleep, stress and behaviour change — anchored on the **~5–10% weight-loss metabolic payoff**. Two often-neglected pillars — the **sleep–stress–cortisol–visceral-fat axis** — are taught as genuinely modifiable levers. Anti-obesity pharmacotherapy and bariatric/metabolic surgery are **signposted only**; detailed T2D and lipid/BP management are handed to their own chapters. The whole chapter is delivered in **non-stigmatising, people-first** language.

## 2. Learning objectives

By the end, a student can…

- [ ] **LO1 — Classify** an Asian adult's weight status using Singapore's BMI action points (**≥23** intervene, **≥27.5** high-risk) plus **waist circumference** (≥90 cm men / ≥80 cm women), and **explain** physiologically why these sit below the WHO ≥25/≥30 cut-offs (higher %body fat and more visceral/ectopic fat at a given BMI; locally quantified by the Yishun Study).
- [ ] **LO2 — Describe** the five metabolic-syndrome components (central adiposity, raised triglycerides, low HDL, raised blood pressure, raised fasting glucose) with their harmonised-2009 thresholds, and **explain** why **≥3 of 5** (no obligatory component) define the syndrome — because they cluster around **insulin resistance and central adiposity**, making MetS a **risk marker** for CVD/T2DM rather than a disease to treat in its own right.
- [ ] **LO3 — Explain** weight gain as cumulative **positive energy balance** modulated by **diet quality** (ultra-processed foods, sugar-sweetened beverages) more than by calorie arithmetic alone, and **outline** the WHO healthy-diet targets and Singapore's translation of them (My Healthy Plate, Nutri-Grade).
- [ ] **LO4 — Justify** why **lifestyle/behavioural modification is first-line**, using the **~5–10% weight-loss** cardiometabolic payoff, and **outline** the escalation ladder — **signposting** (not teaching) where anti-obesity pharmacotherapy and metabolic-bariatric surgery are added by specialists.
- [ ] **LO5 — Outline** how short/poor **sleep** and chronic **stress** intersect with appetite, cortisol and **central adiposity**, and **identify** them as modifiable contributors in a real Singapore patient (e.g. a shift worker).
- [ ] **LO6 — Apply non-stigmatising, weight-inclusive framing** — people-first language, permission-based weight discussion, health-behaviour/metabolic-marker targets — and **recognise** the local ethnic risk gradient without culture-blaming.

*(Verb ceiling: recognise / classify / describe / explain / outline. No "manage with drug X". Pharmacotherapy & surgery = signpost only. Map to `../00-overview/learning-outcomes.md`.)*

## 4. Key concepts to teach (didactic spine)

The spine runs **RECOGNISE the phenotype → understand the clustering biology → understand the drivers → manage lifestyle-first (5 pillars) → frame it without stigma → localise to Singapore**.

1. **Over-nutrition is a pattern in an obesogenic environment, not a moral failing.** Set the non-stigmatising, people-first frame at the outset. *(tie: `rubino2020consensus`, `tomiyama2014cobwebs`)*
2. **BMI is a screening proxy with population-specific cut-offs.** It cannot separate fat from muscle or say *where* fat sits; Singaporean adults carry higher %body fat at a given BMI, so BMI *under-detects* over-fatness locally. *(tie: `chen2021yishun`, `whoExpert2004AsianBMI`)*
3. **Singapore's action points: ≥23 (intervene) and ≥27.5 (high-risk).** Two points below WHO ≥25/≥30, evidence-derived from the 2004 WHO Expert Consultation, operationalised by the HPB-MOH Obesity CPG and reaffirmed in the live Healthier SG BMI-control protocol. *(tie: `hpbmoh2016obesityCPG`, `whoExpert2004AsianBMI`, `sgObesityBMI`)*
4. **Waist circumference is the bedside visceral-fat marker** — Asian cut-offs **≥90 cm men / ≥80 cm women**, lower than Europid/US thresholds, for the same reason as the BMI shift. *(tie: `alberti2009harmonizingMetS`, `sgObesityBMI`)*
5. **The metabolic syndrome = clustering of ≥3 of 5 components** (no obligatory component) around **insulin resistance and central adiposity**; a **risk marker** for CVD/T2DM, not a single disease with a single drug. *(tie: `alberti2009harmonizingMetS`, `kaurMetsDefinitionsReview2014`, `metsDefinitionsControversies2011`)*
6. **Energy balance is the accounting; diet quality is the lever.** Small chronic surpluses accumulate, but *what* is eaten drives overconsumption — **ultra-processed foods cause excess intake even when nutrients are matched**, and **sugar-sweetened beverages** add poorly-compensated liquid calories. *(tie: `hall2019upf`, `lane2024upfumbrella`, `nguyen2023ssb`, `who2020diet`, `aha2021diet`)*
7. **WHO targets, translated for Singapore.** Free sugars <10%E (ideally <5%), sat fat <10%E, trans <1%E, salt <5 g/d → operationalised by **My Healthy Plate** (quarter-quarter-half) and **Nutri-Grade** (A–D beverage grading). *(tie: `who2020diet`, `who2015sugars`, `hpbHealthyPlate`, `hpbNutriGrade`, `shin2023nutrigrade`)*
8. **Lifestyle-first, ~5–10% payoff.** Modest sustained loss yields disproportionate, dose-dependent cardiometabolic benefit; lifestyle is the **cornerstone/base**, with pharmacotherapy and surgery **above** it (signpost only). *(tie: `wing2011modest`, `aceBmiControl2026`, `lee2023obesitylandscape`)*
9. **Activity works partly independently of the scale** — it cuts visceral fat, improves insulin sensitivity and preserves lean mass even when weight barely changes. *(tie: `kolnes2021exercisefat`, `dpp2002`)*
10. **Sleep is a modifiable weight lever.** Short/poor sleep raises energy intake (~+385 kcal/d); *extending* sleep cuts intake (~−270 kcal/d). Teach the appetite-hormone story **directionally** — the reproducible signal is intake, not the contested leptin/ghrelin mechanism. *(tie: `tasali2022sleepextension`, `alkhatib2017`, `spiegel2004`, `taheri2004`, `sleepHormonesMeta2025`, `kohanmoo2024shortsleep`)*
11. **Chronic stress/cortisol biases eating toward energy-dense "comfort" food and channels fat to the viscera** — a coherent mechanistic loop linking stress to the central-adiposity core; stress-reduction (mindfulness) trials for weight are **exploratory**. *(tie: `dallman2003`, `daubenmier2011`)*
12. **Prevention & remission proof-of-concept (with caveats).** DPP: intensive lifestyle cut incident T2D 58% (beating metformin); DiRECT: structured weight-management drove 46% T2D remission — but **surrogate ≠ hard endpoints** (Look AHEAD was neutral on CV events). Deep remission mechanics belong to the Type 2 Diabetes chapter. *(tie: `dpp2002`, `lean2018direct`, `lookahead2013`)*
13. **Weight stigma is a health hazard.** It degrades care, drives avoidance, and (via cortisol/comfort-eating) paradoxically promotes weight gain — the case for weight-inclusive care and people-first language. *(tie: `rubino2020consensus`, `phelan2015impact`, `puhl2023barriers`, `tomiyama2014cobwebs`, `tylka2014weightinclusive`, `batterham2020switchingfocus`, `weghuber2023peoplefirst`, `chue2022stigmasg`, `goff2023sgweightbias`)*
14. **Singapore epidemiology & the ethnic gradient.** Obesity (BMI≥30) rose 10.5%→12.7%; ~1 in 5 sit in the ≥27.5 band; a clear **Malay > Indian > Chinese** obesity/diabetes gradient — framed non-judgementally, motivating the **War on Diabetes** and **Healthier SG**. *(tie: `mohNPHS2024`, `zhang2024bmiSg`, `mohWarOnDiabetes`, `mohHealthierSG`)*

## 6. Singapore context

- **Two number-lines, one message.** Singapore reports *population* obesity at **WHO ≥30 (12.7%, NPHS 2024)** for international comparability, but *clinical* action starts at **≥27.5 (high-risk)** and **≥23 (increased-risk)** — and the ≥27.5 band is a stable ~**1 in 5 adults** (22.8%). Teach the dissociation explicitly: **surveillance threshold ≠ clinical action point**. "12.7% obese" is the newspaper number; ≥27.5/≥23 is the clinic number. The rationale (higher body-fat & visceral fat at a given BMI in Asians) is the Yishun/`whoExpert2004AsianBMI` story.
- **Honest dating of the 2016 CPG.** The Asian cut-offs derive from the **HPB-MOH Obesity CPG 1/2016** (`hpbmoh2016obesityCPG`) and remain current, reaffirmed by the live **Healthier SG BMI-Control protocol** (last updated 16 Apr 2026, `sgObesityBMI`/`aceBmiControl2026`). But the CPG's **pharmacotherapy/surgery sections predate the GLP-1 era** — teach the **cut-offs as current** while flagging the **management content as dated**, superseded by the 2023 *SMJ* treatment-landscape commentary (`lee2023obesitylandscape`).
- **Rising obesity despite rising activity.** NPHS 2024: total physical activity **recovered to 84.7%** yet obesity still rose to 12.7% — a Singapore-grounded argument for **diet-quality-first** framing over "just exercise more."
- **The MetS components at population scale.** NPHS reports *components*, not a bundled MetS prevalence: hypertension **33.8%** and hyperlipidaemia **30.5%** (each ~1 in 3), diabetes **9.1%**. These are the real-world denominators behind the clustering concept.
- **Ethnic risk gradient (framed non-judgementally).** Malay and Indian residents carry markedly higher obesity/diabetes than Chinese (2016: Malay 37.6% > Indian 32.5% > Chinese 13.6% at **WHO BMI≥30**; both ~1.4× vs Chinese, `zhang2024bmiSg`). Drivers are genetic, socio-economic, dietary and environmental — **not moral**; this motivates culturally-tailored, non-stigmatising counselling and the **War on Diabetes** (`mohWarOnDiabetes`). Deep diabetes epidemiology/management → the **Type 2 Diabetes** chapter.
- **Policy levers students should name:** **My Healthy Plate** (`hpbHealthyPlate`), **Nutri-Grade** (`hpbNutriGrade`, with local RCT `shin2023nutrigrade` and national monitoring `moh2023nutrigradeimpact`), the **Healthier Choice Symbol** (`hpbHealthierChoiceSymbol` — voluntary, foods, complements Nutri-Grade for drinks), and **Healthier SG** (`mohHealthierSG`) as the primary-care delivery scaffold that turns "recognise and refer" into a longitudinal Health Plan.
- **Hawker worked examples (energy/quality anchors).** *Energy-dense:* char kway teow **~744 kcal / 38 g fat** (near a whole day's fat), plus Nutri-Grade **C/D** drinks (condensed-milk kopi/teh, bubble tea, soft drinks >10 g sugar/100 ml). *Lower-density swaps* (fit My Healthy Plate): **thunder tea rice, non-fried yong tau foo (more veg/tofu, clear soup), fish-slice bee hoon soup, teochew porridge with lean sides**; drinks → **kopi/teh-O kosong** or water (grade A). These make energy balance and the WHO sugar limits concrete and locally credible (`healthhubHawkerSwaps`).
- **Sleep & shift work are high-yield local levers.** Two-thirds of a Singapore working sample were short sleepers and shift work independently ~tripled poor-sleep odds (`visvalingam2020`, `thach2020`) — relevant to the large healthcare/manufacturing/F&B shift workforce, whose schedules push eating into the late-night **kopitiam supper** window where energy-dense options dominate.

## 7. Pillar intersections

- **nutrition × physical activity.** Activity is not just a calorie-burn add-on: it reduces **visceral** and subcutaneous fat, improves **insulin sensitivity** and preserves **lean mass** during a deficit — even when total weight barely moves (`kolnes2021exercisefat`) — hitting the chapter's central-adiposity/insulin-resistance core. Diet quality remains the larger *weight* lever; pair the two (DPP combined ≥7% loss + ≥150 min/wk, `dpp2002`).
- **nutrition × sleep.** Short/poor sleep pushes **energy intake up** (~+385 kcal/d, no compensatory expenditure, `alkhatib2017`) and tracks with **central obesity** (`kohanmoo2024shortsleep`); *extending* sleep pulls intake **down** (~−270 kcal/d, `tasali2022sleepextension`). Teach the **appetite-hormone** story **directionally** — the Spiegel/Taheri leptin-down/ghrelin-up narrative is echoed observationally but a 2025 RCT meta-analysis found **no significant hormone change** (`sleepHormonesMeta2025`); the reproducible, teachable signal is **intake**, not the contested mediator. Locally, shift work is the concrete driver (`visvalingam2020`, `thach2020`).
- **nutrition × stress (cortisol / comfort food).** Chronic stress and glucocorticoids bias choice toward energy-dense **"comfort" food** and channel fat to the **abdomen/viscera** in a self-reinforcing loop (`dallman2003`) — the mechanistic bridge from stress to the metabolic-syndrome core. Present **stress-reduction (mindfulness) trials for weight honestly as exploratory** (`daubenmier2011`).
- **stigma × stress (the paradox loop).** Weight stigma is *itself* a physiological stressor: it raises cortisol and comfort-eating and thereby promotes weight gain (**COBWEBS**, `tomiyama2014cobwebs`) — so non-stigmatising care is not merely kind, it removes a genuine metabolic stressor. This threads the framing pillar into the sleep-stress-cortisol axis.

## 8. Clinical application & case ideas

**MCQ-only, do-no-harm aware.** Per house rule, per-chapter cases are choice/MCQ; the live AI patient-chat belongs ONLY to the Integrated Cases chapter. *(Log in `../cases/case-bank.md`.)*

**Case A — "Mr Tan, 45, BMI 28, central obesity"** *(flagship classify → cluster → lever hunt)*
- *Stem:* Chinese office worker, BMI **28 kg/m²**, waist **98 cm**, BP **138/86**, fasting glucose slightly raised, low HDL. Daily hawker lunch (char kway teow, kopi-C ×2/day), <5,000 steps, sleeps ~6 h, deadline snacking.
- *Q1 (classify):* Weight status? ✔ "High-risk by Singapore action point (≥27.5) with central obesity by Asian waist cut-off" / ✘ "Normal — BMI <30" (teaches WHO vs Asian number-line; `hpbmoh2016obesityCPG`, `chen2021yishun`).
- *Q2 (MetS tally):* Which findings meet MetS? Central obesity + ↓HDL + ↑BP + ↑FPG = **≥3/5 → meets criteria**; reinforce it's a **risk marker**, not a diagnosis to drug (`alberti2009harmonizingMetS`).
- *Q3 (multi-pillar lever hunt):* Identify nutrition (energy-dense hawker meals, sugary kopi), activity (sedentary), **sleep** (6 h), **stress** (snacking). ✔ multi-pillar plan / ✘ "start a weight-loss drug now" → feedback: **lifestyle is first-line; pharmacotherapy is specialist/future learning** (`lee2023obesitylandscape`).
- *Q4 (first-line plan):* Realistic swaps (half-rice + veg, **kopi-O kosong**, lunchtime walk, sleep target), **~5–10% loss** goal (`wing2011modest`, `aceBmiControl2026`); permission-based, person-first framing.

**Case B — "Ms Sarah, 32, rotating-shift nurse"** *(sleep-stress axis + non-stigmatising framing)*
- *Stem:* BMI 26, waist trending up, night-shift stress-snacking, irregular sleep, frequent bubble tea (Nutri-Grade **D**).
- *Q1 (mechanism):* Best links short/disrupted sleep + stress to intake? ✔ "↑ energy intake and cortisol-driven cravings; taught directionally" / ✘ "definitely ↓ leptin proves it" (honesty: hormone mechanism contested, `sleepHormonesMeta2025`; intake signal solid, `alkhatib2017`, `thach2020`).
- *Q2 (prioritise pillar):* ✔ sleep/circadian stabilisation + planned night-shift meals / ✘ "just eat less / more willpower" (upstream driver beats willpower messaging).
- *Q3 (beverage swap):* Use **Nutri-Grade** as a decision aid → choose grade A/B or water (`hpbNutriGrade`).
- *Do-no-harm trap:* Selecting shame/blame counselling → **stigma feedback loop** feedback (COBWEBS, `tomiyama2014cobwebs`; local `chue2022stigmasg`).

**Case C — "Mdm Devi, 58, ethnic-gradient framing"** *(non-stigmatising, culturally-aware)*
- *Stem:* Indian retiree, BMI 27, strong family diabetes history, feels blamed by past clinicians and avoids reviews.
- *Q1:* Why is she higher-risk at BMI 27? ✔ "Asian body-fat distribution + local ethnic gradient; risk is multifactorial, not moral" / ✘ "her diet is a personal failing" (`zhang2024bmiSg`, `goff2023sgweightbias`).
- *Q2 (framing):* Best opening? ✔ "Ask permission before discussing weight; person-first language; agree a health goal" / ✘ comment on appearance (`batterham2020switchingfocus`, `weghuber2023peoplefirst`).
- *Do-no-harm:* Recognise that a stigmatising encounter drives **care avoidance** (`phelan2015impact`, `puhl2023barriers`).

## 9. Common misconceptions & pitfalls

- **"BMI 28 is fine — obesity is BMI ≥30."** → Conflates WHO with Singapore action points; **≥27.5** is the local high-risk threshold, **≥23** already triggers intervention.
- **"BMI measures fat directly / is enough on its own."** → BMI is a proxy that can't separate fat from muscle or locate it; pair with **waist** (Asian cut-offs). Singaporeans carry higher %body fat at a given BMI (`chen2021yishun`).
- **"The 2016 CPG is the current standard for everything in obesity."** → Cut-offs persist, but its **management** content is pre-GLP-1; date it honestly.
- **"Metabolic syndrome is one disease you treat with one drug."** → It's a **clustering (≥3/5)** around insulin resistance/central adiposity — a **risk marker**, and local SG data even suggest *several distinct entities* (`angMetsSingaporeNHS2005`).
- **"Weight is purely calories in/out and willpower."** → Diet **quality** (UPF, SSBs), sleep, stress and environment modulate intake and fat distribution; framing matters and stigma harms.
- **"A diet drink solves a sugary-drink habit."** → WHO 2023 conditionally advises **against non-sugar sweeteners** for weight control; answer is water/unsweetened + behaviour change (`who2023nss`).
- **"Just exercise more and the weight comes off."** → Nationally, activity recovered (84.7%) while obesity rose; activity's biggest wins are **visceral fat/insulin sensitivity**, and **diet quality** is the larger weight lever (`mohNPHS2024`, `kolnes2021exercisefat`).
- **"Start drugs/surgery early."** → First-year stops at **lifestyle-first**; pharmacotherapy/bariatric surgery are **signposted** specialist options (`lee2023obesitylandscape`).
- **"Trials prove diet cures diabetes for everyone."** → **DiRECT's 46% remission** applies to **early (≤6 y), non-insulin, BMI 27–45** patients in a structured programme, untested in Asian primary care; **DPP's 58%** came from an **intensive 16-session programme**, not one clinic visit; and **Look AHEAD** improved surrogate markers but was **NEUTRAL on hard CV events** (stopped for futility). Surrogate ≠ hard endpoint.
- **"Shaming motivates weight loss."** → It does the opposite — stigma is a stressor that ↑cortisol/eating and promotes weight gain and **care avoidance** (`tomiyama2014cobwebs`, `phelan2015impact`).
- **"Sleep loss reliably drops leptin and raises ghrelin — that's the mechanism."** → Teach **directionally**: a 2025 RCT meta-analysis found **no significant hormone change**; the robust signal is increased **intake** (`sleepHormonesMeta2025`, `alkhatib2017`).
