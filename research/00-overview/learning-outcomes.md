# Programme learning outcomes

> Derived by the research pass (2026-06-30). Chapter-level objectives live in
> [`curriculum-map.md`](curriculum-map.md) and each chapter dossier; tag them up to these
> programme outcomes in each dossier's §2.

By the end of the first-year nutrition curriculum, a student can:

1. **Nutrition science.** Describe the core principles of nutrition science (energy balance,
   macronutrients, micronutrients) and explain how dietary patterns influence the risk of obesity,
   cardiovascular disease, hypertension and type 2 diabetes, drawing on WHO, HPB/MOH and
   international guidance.
2. **Assessment.** Take a basic diet history and dietary-intake screen in a brief clinical
   encounter, identify nutrients of likely deficit or excess, and interpret HPB My Healthy Plate,
   Nutri-Grade and standard food labels.
3. **Guideline literacy.** Locate, compare and apply Singapore (HPB/MOH/ACE) and international
   (WHO/ACLM/ADA/AHA) dietary guidance, recognising the common messages shared across global
   dietary guidelines.
4. **Evidence appraisal.** Critically appraise basic nutrition evidence at an introductory level,
   distinguishing evidence-based dietary guidance from fad diets and unproven supplements.
5. **Integrated frame.** Explain nutrition within an integrated lifestyle-medicine frame,
   describing how physical activity, sleep, stress, alcohol and social connection interact
   bidirectionally with diet to shape metabolic health.
6. **Counselling.** Apply a structured brief-counselling approach to a simple dietary-change
   scenario using the 5A's, introductory motivational-interviewing skills and SMART goal-setting,
   with cultural tailoring to local (e.g. hawker-food) eating patterns.
7. **Referral & teamwork.** Recognise the boundaries of the first-year clinician's role and
   demonstrate when and how to refer a patient to a registered dietitian for medical nutrition
   therapy, working within an interprofessional team.
8. **Policy & populations.** Relate national nutrition policy (War on Diabetes, Nutri-Grade,
   Healthier Dining Programme, National Nutrition Survey 2022) to population dietary risk and the
   food environment, including ethnic-specific cardiometabolic risk in Singapore.
9. **Self-care & professionalism.** Reflect on the practitioner's own dietary and lifestyle health
   as part of professional development and role-modelling.
