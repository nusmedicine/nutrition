## Design logic and frameworks

This first-year curriculum is **nutrition-led** but deliberately taught inside an **integrated
lifestyle-medicine (LM) frame**, so that diet is presented as the hub that every other LM pillar
(physical activity, sleep, stress, risky substances, social connection) either supports or
undermines. It is **Singapore-first**: every applied chapter is anchored to HPB/MOH instruments
(My Healthy Plate, Nutri-Grade, National Nutrition Survey 2022, Healthier Dining Programme, War on
Diabetes, Asian BMI cut-offs, ACE Clinical Guidances), then complemented by international guidance
(WHO Healthy Diet; ADA Standards of Care; AHA 2021; Dietary Guidelines for Americans) and landmark
trials.

**Competency spine.** The chapter map is built on the convergent competency domains synthesised
across five frameworks (below). They converge on a first-year spine: **nutrition science → dietary
assessment → evidence literacy → dietary patterns/prescription basics → behaviour-change
counselling → interprofessional referral.** The AfN eight-topic taxonomy supplies the
foundational-knowledge backbone; the Nutrition in Medicine (NIM, UNC) titles supply a complementary
applied axis; the NHLBI/IOM content list and Harvard's longitudinal-theme model justify sequencing
nutrition science early and embedding it longitudinally rather than as an isolated block.

**Sequencing.** Following the IOM/NAS guideline and Harvard integration model, the curriculum runs
**foundations → applications → counselling/behaviour change**.

**Pillar intersections.** Each chapter explicitly flags where nutrition meets the other LM pillars,
using mechanistic intersections: nutrition×physical activity (DPP), nutrition×sleep (insulin
sensitivity, appetite hormones), nutrition×circadian (time-restricted eating), nutrition×stress
(cortisol, comfort food), nutrition×alcohol (calories + metabolic toxicity), and nutrition×social
connection (support and adherence). This teaches first-years that multi-pillar interventions
outperform single-behaviour approaches.

**First-year calibration.** Depth is held at building **nutrition literacy and basic counselling
skills**, not specialist clinical management: take a diet history, read a Nutri-Grade/food label,
recognise healthy dietary patterns, distinguish evidence from fads, apply a structured
brief-counselling scaffold (5A's + introductory MI + SMART goals), and recognise the limits of the
role and **when to refer to a dietitian**. Therapeutic detail (drug titration, enteral/parenteral
protocols, full ACE pharmacotherapy algorithms) is signposted as future learning.

**Counselling models.** Motivational interviewing (learnable, improves adherence), the 5A's brief
scaffold (Ask–Advise–Assess–Assist–Arrange), SMART goal-setting as the action engine, and the
Transtheoretical/Stages-of-Change model taught with **scientific humility** (useful heuristic,
mixed weight-loss outcome evidence per the Cochrane review).

## Competency frameworks referenced
- **ACLM Lifestyle Medicine Core Competencies (2022 Update)** — 10 domains; Domain 3 = Nutrition Science, Assessment & Prescription.
- **Lianov & Johnson (2010, JAMA)** — 15 physician competencies for lifestyle medicine across 5 domains (Leadership, Knowledge, Assessment, Management, Use of office/community).
- **Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics (2017)** — interprofessional/team-based nutrition care position.
- **Van Horn / NNEdPro (2019)** — "Building Competencies" for nutrition in medical education.
- **AfN UK Undergraduate Curriculum in Nutrition for Medical Doctors (2021)** — eight topic areas A–H, mapped to core competencies & graduation fundamentals.
- **Nutrition in Medicine (NIM, UNC); NHLBI/IOM content list; Harvard longitudinal-theme model** — for topic taxonomy and sequencing.
