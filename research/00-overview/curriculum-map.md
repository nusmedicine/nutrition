## Design philosophy
1. **A firm metabolic & physiological basis first**, sequenced on one principle: *substrate defined
   before its fate; absorbed before metabolised; pathways before integration; system before its regulation.*
2. **Organise by the patient (health → disease).** After the science (I), the spine follows *who you are
   advising*: needs vary across the **life stages** (II) → advising the **healthy** individual (III, incl.
   prevention) → advising the **chronically ill** individual (IV, maintaining health with disease) →
   **integration** (V). Over- and under-nutrition are taught together as the two tails of malnutrition.
3. **Cases in three tiers** — embedded per-chapter (deterministic), a case bank, and a **capstone**
   integrative-cases chapter (home of the optional **LLM patient**; planned now, built later).
4. **Teach the science basis, and show it (2026-07-06 directive).** Part IV (conditions) chapters carry a
   **basic pathophysiology** beat (the disease mechanism, building on the Part I owners by name — e.g. obesity →
   visceral/ectopic fat → adipose inflammation & adipokine shift → insulin resistance + leptin/appetite
   dysregulation → the MetS cluster); Part III (advising the healthy) chapters carry the **relevant
   physiology/biochemistry background** needed to understand the nutrition material; and every chapter uses
   **figures/photos where they teach**. Codified in **AUTHOR §11f**.

## Part I — sequencing, contents & overlap ownership *(research-backed)*

**Order rationale:** *substrate must be defined before its fate; molecules absorbed before metabolised;
individual pathways before their integration; the integrated system before its regulation.* The
"energy-first" opening (vs the canonical North-American digestion-first spine) is a deliberate
UK/Nutrition-Society + lifestyle-medicine choice — it eases students in with the least biochem-heavy
content. *(Note, 2026-07-04: **Ch.6 is now self-contained** — it teaches the core pathways + their interconnection at map depth in its Part A, then integrates them in Part B; no parallel biochem course is assumed.)*
> ✅ **Scheduling constraint RESOLVED (2026-07-04):** Ch.6 no longer assumes a synchronised biochem
> course — it **carries the pathways itself** as a self-contained **Part A (the metabolic map, at
> interconnection-map depth)**, then does the fed/fasted integration in Part B. (Decision: the pathways
> foundation is a hard prerequisite for the inter-organ integration, so it lives in-chapter.)
>
> 🔀 **Microbiome placement (decided 2026-07-03, evidence-backed).** The Gut Microbiome is a **standalone
> chapter placed after Integrative Metabolism (Ch.6) and before Appetite (Ch.8)** — i.e. **Ch.7**,
> swapped with Metabolism from the prior order. Rationale: a rich, trans-disciplinary chapter belongs
> *after* its prerequisites (prerequisite sequencing + cognitive-load theory + Harden's integration
> ladder), so its SCFA metabolic fates *build on* Ch.6 instead of forward-referencing it, and Appetite
> (Ch.8) can then integrate the microbiome's satiety contribution while still closing the loop with
> Energy Balance (Ch.2). Surveyed textbooks embed only a *shallow* microbiome topic early with digestion;
> our deliberate *standalone* treatment earns the later, integrative slot. (Survey: 5 nutrition + 6
> physiology/biochem texts + med-ed sequencing pedagogy, 2026-07-03.)

### Part I authoring principles (decided 2026-06-30)
- **Nutrient chapters are function-led, not laundry lists.** Macros (Ch.3) & Micros (Ch.4) stay
  *process-organized* but taught by **function & clinical relevance**, with a high-level functional
  preview (e.g. "carbs → glucose → fuel; *quality* matters") and a recurring **"follow the nutrient"
  through-line** that Digestion (Ch.5) and Integrative Metabolism (Ch.6) pick up. *(Not the
  nutrient-vertical / Gropper model.)*
- **Dietary reference intakes:** teach the **concept once** (EAR/RDA/AI/UL/AMDR + limits) in the
  nutrient foundations; **embed specific values** per nutrient; provide a **reference appendix/table**.
  Not a standalone chapter.
- **Diet history** is a **Part II clinical skill** (Ch.11), with an **early "audit your own diet"
  taster** in Part I so students practise as they learn.
- **Appetite & Weight Regulation (Ch.8) stays the Part I capstone**, explicitly **bookended with
  Energy Balance (Ch.2)** — *frame ↔ regulation*, closing the loop. (Not merged into Energy Balance;
  not folded into Obesity.)

### Chapter contents (the didactic spine of each)
- **2 · Energy Balance & Body Composition** — energy-balance equation, units/Atwater; TEE components (BMR, TEF, EAT+NEAT); measuring expenditure (calorimetry, DLW, RQ*); body-composition models & assessment (BMI, BIA, DXA, waist); +/- energy balance; BMR determinants. *(*RQ = forward-referenced to Ch.6.)*
- **3 · Macronutrients** — CHO classification, GI/GL; fibre types (structure only); lipids (FA classes, TG, phospholipids, cholesterol, EFAs) + **name** lipoprotein classes*; protein (AAs, quality PDCAAS/DIAAS), N balance; functions/sources/yields; DRIs/AMDR. *(*lipoprotein assembly→Ch.5, interconversion→Ch.6.)*
- **4 · Micronutrients, Hydration & Electrolytes** — fat- & water-soluble vitamins; major & trace minerals; body water (ICF/ECF), fluid balance, osmolality, Na/K, acid–base; hydration assessment; DRIs/bioavailability/UL.
- **5 · Digestion & Absorption** ✨ — GI anatomy & accessory organs; motility; chemical digestion (salivary→brush-border); **GI hormones as secretory/motility regulators only** (gastrin, secretin, CCK, GIP, motilin); absorptive mechanisms & enterocyte/villus; nutrient-specific absorption; **bile/micelles/enterohepatic circulation (owner)**; brief colonic-fermentation mention.
- **6 · Integrative Metabolism (Fed/Fasted)** ✨ — **two layers, self-contained (2026-07-04 decision — now teaches the pathway basics, not just integration).** **Part A = the metabolic map** (core pathways + how they interconnect at *interconnection-map* depth: glycolysis, gluconeogenesis, glycogen, PPP, β-oxidation, lipogenesis, ketogenesis, TCA, oxphos, amino-acid/nitrogen; the 5 crossroads + 4 one-way valves; reciprocal regulation; the energy currency). **Part B = the dynamic integration** — fed state (insulin: glycogenesis/lipogenesis/storage); fasted state (glucagon/epinephrine: glycogenolysis/lipolysis); starvation adaptations; hormonal regulation; inter-organ fuel exchange (Cori/glucose-alanine); RQ; exercise.
- **7 · The Gut Microbiome** ✨ — microbiota composition/development; **fibre→SCFA fermentation (owner)**; microbial vitamin synthesis; prebiotics/probiotics/fermented foods; diet as modulator; **gut–brain axis *microbial mechanism* at recognition level (owner)**. *Placed after Integrative Metabolism (Ch.6), so SCFA metabolic fates build on it directly; trim only the appetite/satiety payoff (→ Ch.8) and disease framing (obesity/T2D → Part III) to bracketed callbacks.* (Agrees with the overlap-ownership table below.)
- **8 · Appetite & Weight Regulation** ✨ — hunger/satiety/satiation; hypothalamic control (arcuate NPY/AgRP vs POMC/CART); **adiposity signals leptin/insulin**; **gut peptides' satiety role** (ghrelin, PYY and amylin introduced here; CCK and GLP-1 met earlier in Ch.5); set-point & defence of weight; hedonic/reward eating; adaptive thermogenesis after weight loss. *Closes the loop back to Ch.2.*
